## Q98 — Lorentz coupling: wind × magnetic field deflects velocity
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Pre-Q98 the three M1b state fields lived in three
sealed compartments: `Wind` (Q91) updated `(v_q, v_r)` from
temperature gradients; `Electromagnetism` diffused `charge`;
`Magnetism` (Q94) shaped `(B_q, B_r)` from a planetary dipole.
None of them talked to each other. Real physics has them all
coupled through the Lorentz force `F = q · v × B`, which
deflects the motion of charged particles in a magnetic field.
Geostrophic-style wind deflection, ionospheric currents, aurora
geometry, and the Q-doc-listed "Lorentz coupling on charge"
follow-up from Q94 all depend on this coupling.

**Decision:** Add a `Lorentz` law that runs each macro-step
after `Magnetism` (so it reads the freshest `|B|`) and applies
the per-cell `q · v × B` kick to the wind velocity:

```text
  Δv_q[i] = +lorentz_k · dt · charge[i] · v_r[i] · |B|[i]
  Δv_r[i] = -lorentz_k · dt · charge[i] · v_q[i] · |B|[i]
```

`|B|[i]` reads the cached `magnetic_magnitude` slice from Q95.
The 2D cross-product treats the magnetic field as having a
vertical-axis component proxied by the cell's total field
magnitude — a simplification over true 3D `(B_q, B_r, B_z)`
physics, but the *direction* of the deflection (rotational
between `v_q` and `v_r`) matches real Lorentz qualitatively.
Real dipole physics has the vertical component peak at the
magnetic poles where horizontal magnitude is weakest, so the
proxy is wrong at the poles but right at mid-latitudes (which
is where most wind flows anyway). Refining to a separate
`B_z` field is the deferred follow-up.

`integrate_civ_step` gains an 12th `lorentz: Option<&Lorentz>`
parameter; `build_laws` returns a 10-tuple including
`Lorentz::earth_like()` (`lorentz_k = 1e-5`).

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Physics as close to real as possible:* Lorentz is *the*
  coupling between three fundamental fields (charge, velocity,
  magnetic). Without it, you have parallel single-particle
  physics that never interact. Now charged regions move
  differently from neutral regions, and the magnetic dipole's
  influence reaches into wind dynamics.
- *Least hardcoded values:* one tunable coefficient
  (`lorentz_k`). Per-cell behaviour is purely the product of
  three existing state fields.
- *Determinism:* per-cell read of `charge`, `v_q`, `v_r`, `|B|`;
  per-cell write of `v_q`, `v_r`. No pair iteration. No
  interior mutability. No state-dependent branching beyond
  short-circuit on zero-charge / zero-field cells.
- *Closes the M1b loop:* the original Q43 coupling-order plan
  named `fluid → heat`, `fluid → EM` couplings as M1b
  follow-ups. Q91 supplied the velocity field; Q94 supplied
  the magnetic vector; Q98 is the genuine coupling between
  them. Three previously-isolated fields now form a single
  consistent system.

**Numerical care.**
- The discrete-time Euler integration of the Lorentz rotation
  has `|v|² → (1 + s²) · |v|²` per tick, where
  `s = lorentz_k · dt · q · |B|`. With `lorentz_k = 1e-5`,
  `dt = 1`, `q ≤ 50`, `|B| ≤ 50`: `s ≤ 0.025`, so `|v|² grows`
  by `≤ 0.06 %`/tick. Over 24,000 ticks this would compound to
  4× growth without other constraints — but `Wind`'s 30 %/tick
  friction (Q91) damps velocity faster than Lorentz's
  rotational growth, keeping the system stable.
- The Euler scheme is also unstable (rotation doesn't preserve
  norm) when `s > 1`. `lorentz_k = 1e-5` keeps `s` well below
  1 for any plausible charge / field combination.
- Implicit-symplectic rotation (Boris pusher) would conserve
  `|v|` exactly even at large `s`. Worth doing if the
  follow-up Q-decisions push charge densities into the
  thousands; for now the explicit Euler is cleaner and
  Q32.32-friendly.

**Lives in:** `sim/physics/src/lorentz.rs` (the law),
`sim/physics/src/lib.rs` (re-export),
`sim/physics/src/orchestration.rs` (wired after `Magnetism`,
before chemistry; `integrate_civ_step` takes
`lorentz: Option<&Lorentz>` so deterministic micro-tests can
pass `None`),
`sim/core/src/lib.rs` (`build_laws` returns a 10-tuple with
`Lorentz::earth_like()`).

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- *Separate vertical B field.* Real Lorentz needs the true
  vertical component of `B`. Lands when Q94's magnetic vector
  grows to `(B_q, B_r, B_z)` (the magnitude-proxy here is
  wrong at the magnetic poles).
- *Boris pusher.* Implicit-symplectic rotation preserves `|v|`
  exactly. Worth doing if charge densities grow large or if
  `lorentz_k` needs to be cranked up.
- *Charge advection along velocity.* Currently EM only diffuses
  charge; wind doesn't carry it. Real plasma physics has bulk
  charge transport. Symmetric to Q91's heat advection but on
  the charge field; would close the second half of the
  `wind ↔ charge` coupling.
- *Aurora recognition templates.* Now that Lorentz couples
  charge and field, regions near magnetic poles (high `|B|`
  + accumulated charge) become real-physics-recognisable
  aurora zones. Authoring lands when the recognition layer
  grows a polar-spike template.
- *Lightning discharge along field lines.* `Electromagnetism`
  currently discharges charge at threshold straight to ground.
  Real lightning bolts follow the path of least resistance,
  which the magnetic field shapes. Lands when discharge becomes
  a directed event rather than an in-place dissipation.
