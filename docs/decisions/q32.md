## Q32 — Symbolic vs quantitative discovery
**Status:** Decided (retargeted by Q40)
**Context:** With the symbolic discovery model from Q6, "depth =
quantify" was just a flag — civs held tokens for relations, not actual
quantitative knowledge. Options were: (A) stay symbolic, (B) annotate
confirmed relations with functional-form templates, (C) generate
observations from synthetic true-formulas and let civs propose, fit,
and refine.
**Decision:** Adopt C. Named figures propose functional forms from a
small vocabulary, fit them against accumulated data points, and confirm
when residual is below an intelligence-scaled tolerance. Refinement
allows superseding previously confirmed forms. Wrong hypotheses are
first-class state. Determinism via Q36 arithmetic. With Q40, the
*data points being fit* come from sampling real physics state through
the recognition layer, not from authored `true_formula` fields. The
fitting machinery is unchanged; the source of truth is now the actual
simulation.
**Lives in:** `docs/civ.md` Discovery section,
`docs/architecture.md` Build-time decisions → Deterministic arithmetic.
