## Q111 — Substrate-neutral solvent-cycle template names
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Q102 added 4 recognition templates (`tropical_moist`,
`dry_zone`, `storm_cell`, `windy_strait`) for the Q90-Q101
physics. The signatures read solvent-agnostic fields
(`Field::Substance(Vapour)`, `WaterDepth`, climate bands), so
they fire correctly on any substrate. But the *names* assumed
Earth-water phenomenology. A civ on Titan whose methane vapour
exceeded the Hot-band threshold would correctly trigger
`tropical_moist` — but the label is wrong; the civ is observing
methane haze, not water humidity.

The Earth-bias mismatch only affects naming (and any downstream
report or recognition-tag rendering), not behaviour. But "least
hardcoded values" means the labels shouldn't assume Earth
either.

**Decision:** Rename the four Q102 templates to substrate-neutral
labels that describe the *solvent cycle* abstractly:

| Q102 (Earth-named) | Q111 (substrate-neutral) | What fires |
|---|---|---|
| `tropical_moist` | `solvent_humid_band` | high Vapour in Hot band |
| `dry_zone` | `desiccated_band` | low solvent + low Vapour in ProductiveBand |
| `storm_cell` | `condensation_storm` | high Vapour in Cold band |
| `windy_strait` | `windy_strait` | unchanged (pure-physics, no substrate assumption) |

Template IDs (36-39) and signatures unchanged — only names and
doc comments. `windy_strait` was already substrate-neutral
(reads `Field::WindMagnitude` only) so it stays.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Least hardcoded values:* the only Earth assumption removed is
  the *name*. The signatures were already substrate-agnostic; the
  rename matches behaviour to label.
- *Civs on any substrate get correct recognition:* a Titan civ
  observing high methane vapour in its Hot band now sees
  `solvent_humid_band` — neutral term that whatever its language
  describes, isn't implying water specifically. Same for
  ammonia, silicate, hydrocarbon worlds.
- *Backwards-compat is fine:* Q102 names had no callers outside
  the recognition library itself (verified by grep). Reports and
  digests render template IDs / tags, not names directly, so no
  downstream breakage.
- *Q84/Q87 substrate-specific templates still complement:*
  `silicate_resonance` (id 29), `methane_seep` (id 30),
  `ammoniacal_storm` (id 31), `cryo_lake` (id 33) etc. continue
  to fire on their substrate-specific signatures. Q111's renames
  cover the *general* solvent cycle; Q84/Q87 cover the
  *substrate-specific* phenomena.

**Lives in:** `sim/recognition/src/lib.rs` — rename + doc
update on the four Q102 templates.

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- *Substrate-specific solvent-band variants.* Even with
  substrate-neutral names, each substrate could get its own
  variant (e.g. `methane_solvent_humid_band` with thresholds
  calibrated for cold-Hydrocarbon vapour magnitudes). Adds
  detail without removing the neutral fall-back.
- *Civ-language rendering.* When the report layer renders
  these tags into prose, "solvent_humid_band" is awkward
  English. A small render-side rule could substitute "humid"
  on aqueous, "haze" on hydrocarbon, etc.
