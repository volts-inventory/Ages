## Q52 — Use real SI units and physical constants throughout
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Should have been raised during M1a planning but wasn't.
M0–M2 implemented physics with arbitrary "Earth-like" stylised
units (temperature 0–30, pressure 0–3, etc.) for fixed-point-
arithmetic convenience and faster iteration. That choice silently
contradicts the project's premise — civs deriving real physics
from real observations only lands if the observations are in real
units with real constants. Caught two milestones late while wiring
planet-derived law coefficients.
**Decision:** Convert to SI units everywhere. Use real physical
constants. Use real equation forms (Clausius-Clapeyron for boiling
point, Stefan-Boltzmann for radiation, Fourier's law for heat
conduction, Coulomb's law structure for EM). Implement
deterministic fixed-point `sqrt`, `ln`, `exp`, `pow` in `sim/arith`
to support the equation forms that need them.

**Concrete units:**
- Temperature: Kelvin (273.15 = 0°C)
- Distance: metres
- Pressure: Pascals (101 325 = 1 atm)
- Gravity: m/s² (Earth = 9.81)
- Time: seconds (civ-sim tick = 31 536 000)
- Mass: kg, energy: J

**Real constants now used:**
- Water freeze 273.15 K, boil 373.15 K at 1 atm
- L_fusion 334 000 J/kg, L_vap 2 257 000 J/kg
- c_p_water 4186 J/(kg·K)
- σ (Stefan-Boltzmann) 5.67×10⁻⁸ W/(m²·K⁴)
- R (gas constant) 8.314 J/(mol·K)

**What stays simplified** (out of scope even with this pivot):
spherical geometry (still flat-torus hex), 3D fluid dynamics (still
2D shallow-water), full-GCM climate fidelity. The Goldberg sphere,
real shallow-water momentum, and atmospheric circulation are still
escape-hatch deferrals.
**Lives in:** `sim/arith` (transcendentals), `sim/physics` (law
coefficients in SI), `sim/world` (planet sampling in SI),
`sim/recognition` (template thresholds in SI),
`docs/architecture.md` Physics engine.
