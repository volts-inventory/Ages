## Q69 — Catastrophe severity / cadence from planet substrate
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Q60 catastrophes ship with flat per-kind constants:
disease drops 30% of pop, volcanic fires every prime-period
ticks regardless of crust, ice age severity is uniform across
planets. Q64 made the catastrophes hit specific cells, but the
*magnitude* and *frequency* are still planet-blind. Black Death
hit a Lush biosphere harder than it would have on a tundra;
Basaltic-crust planets erupt more than Hydrocarbon or
Piezoelectric ones; ice ages are existential on cold planets,
incidental on hot ones.

Q69 derives severity + cadence from existing seed-sampled inputs
(biosphere class, crust enum, mean_temperature) — no new
authoring, no new traits.

**Decision:** Three derivations on three catastrophe kinds
(volcanic, disease, ice age). Asteroid stays uniform (cosmic
rocks don't care about planet biosphere). Solar flare stays
gated on stellar_luminosity + magnetosphere (already done).

### 1. Disease severity scales with biosphere

Biosphere richness drives pathogen diversity. Lush worlds host
more microbes; Sparse worlds host fewer; None worlds host
practically none.

```
disease_severity_factor:
  None              → 0.20
  Sparse            → 0.60
  Lush              → 1.00 (Earth-like baseline; current default)
  HyperBiodiverse   → 1.50
```

Final disease pop-loss fraction = `DISEASE_POP_LOSS × factor`.
A HyperBiodiverse civ loses ~45% of pop in its densest cell
during a plague (vs current 30%); a Sparse civ loses ~18%.
Diseases on None-biosphere worlds are basically a footnote —
the species' immune system has nothing to fight.

### 2. Volcanic frequency scales with crust

Crust composition drives mantle activity. Basaltic crust
(Earth-like) is the calibration baseline. Hydrocarbon-rich
crust (oil/gas) often correlates with high volcanic activity
in real planetology. Piezoelectric and rare-earth crusts are
typically older, more stable.

The volcanic firing trigger is a `charge × temperature` cell
threshold (already physical). Q69 adjusts the *cooldown
window* — Basaltic civs experience volcanic recurrence at the
calibration cadence; other crusts stretch the window:

```
volcanic_cooldown_factor:
  Basaltic         → 1.00 (baseline; current default)
  Hydrocarbon      → 0.80 (more frequent — 20% shorter cooldown)
  Piezoelectric    → 1.40 (less frequent)
  Ferrous          → 1.10 (slightly less frequent)
  RareEarth        → 1.50 (much less frequent — old stable crust)
```

`effective_volcanic_cooldown = VOLCANIC_COOLDOWN_TICKS × factor`.

### 3. Ice age severity scales with mean temperature

A cold planet (mean_temp ≤ 263 K, -10 °C) experiences an ice
age as existential — half the cells freeze solid, food security
craters across the civ. A warm planet (mean_temp ≥ 290 K) shrugs
off an ice age as "a chilly century." Q69 scales pop-loss by
how far below 273 K the planet sits:

```
chill_factor = max(1.0, (273 - mean_temperature_k) / 20)
ice_age_pop_loss_effective = ICE_AGE_POP_LOSS × chill_factor
```

A planet at 263 K (just below freezing) gets `chill_factor =
max(1.0, 0.5) = 1.0` (baseline). A planet at 243 K (-30 °C)
gets `chill_factor = 1.5` (50% worse). Above 273 K, factor
stays at 1.0 (warm planets are unaffected by the modifier).

### Wiring

New helpers in `sim/civ/catastrophe.rs`:
- `disease_severity_factor(biosphere) -> Real`
- `volcanic_cooldown_factor(crust) -> Real`
- `ice_age_severity_factor(mean_temperature_k) -> Real`

`check_and_apply` reads each one and applies to the appropriate
fraction / cooldown.

### Determinism contract

All factors are pure functions of seed-derived planet fields
(biosphere, crust enum, mean_temperature_q32 → factor in Q32.32
arithmetic). Same seed = same catastrophe severity.

### Why this respects the project goals

- *Emergence over authoring:* a HyperBiodiverse civ doesn't
  have "harder plagues" because someone tagged it — it has
  harder plagues because more biosphere = more pathogens, a
  real biological fact.
- *Different worlds, different sciences:* a civ on a cold
  Hydrocarbon-crust planet has a fundamentally different
  catastrophe profile (frequent volcanic + brutal ice ages +
  mild plague) than a civ on a warm RareEarth-crust
  HyperBiodiverse planet (rare volcanic + mild ice age + fierce
  plagues). Each civilization's narrative is shaped by its
  planetary substrate.
- *Quantitative depth:* catastrophe magnitude becomes a
  measurable per-seed variable, not a flat constant.

### Lives in

`sim/civ/catastrophe.rs` — three new helper functions; revised
`check_and_apply` for disease + volcanic + ice age. Uniform
behaviour for asteroid + solar flare preserved.
