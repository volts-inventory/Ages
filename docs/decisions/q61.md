## Q61 — Buildings as a sim primitive (settlement-tier persistence multiplier)
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Settlements ship today as a render-only artifact: the
post-run report buckets each civ's claimed cells into BFS rings from
the founder centroid and labels them capital / town / village /
hamlet. The simulation itself doesn't know about buildings — they
have no semantic role and don't feed back into any sim mechanic.

PLANNING.md's deferred list has carried "Buildings as a sim
primitive" since the settlement-rendering pass landed, with the
explicit gating note that a real building-as-primitive feature
needs a Q-decision on what buildings *do* in the recognition +
knowledge model. `sim/civ/transmission.rs`'s docstring already
reserves the slot: "Cultural-distance and per-artifact persistence-
tier folds in once Q24 + an artifact infrastructure land."

Q61's question: what do buildings *do* in the simulation, and how
do they feed back into existing mechanics without authoring a new
top-down tier ladder?

**Decision:** Buildings act as **knowledge-persistence anchors**
that scale Q50 inter-civ transmission. A civ's *peak settlement
scale* — the high-water mark of its `claimed_cells.len()` over its
lifetime — maps to a multiplier on the Q50 comprehension score.
Civs that grew large enough to support a multi-tier settlement
network leave more knowledge for their successors than civs that
died at hamlet scale.

**Why peak, not current.** A civ that grew huge then shrank to
one cell during a long decline still built monumental archives at
its peak. Those archives don't vaporise during contraction — they
remain in the physical landscape for successor civs to find. Using
peak captures "how much knowledge infrastructure did this society
ever build" rather than "how much did it have at the moment of
collapse".

**Why claimed_cells count, not BFS-ring depth.** The render-side
per-cell tier labels (capital / town / village / hamlet) are a
useful *display*, but for transmission what matters is the *total
network scale*: how many physical sites accumulated written
knowledge across the territory. `claimed_cells.len()` is the
direct sim-side signal; ring depth is a derived presentation of
the same data.

**Multiplier ladder** (Q28 tuning umbrella):

| peak_claimed_cells | Settlement scale | persistence_mult |
|---:|---|---:|
| 0–1 | Capital only (oral tradition) | 0.85 |
| 2–5 | + towns (codified writing emerges) | 1.00 |
| 6–15 | + villages (mass-distributed writing) | 1.15 |
| 16+ | + hamlets (full network, archives span territory) | 1.30 |

The 0.85 floor below 1.0 is deliberate: a single-cell civ with no
satellite settlements and no surrounding population centres is
materially worse at preserving knowledge across a collapse boundary
than a multi-cell civ that left written records distributed across
a territory. The 1.30 ceiling at the top tier mirrors the existing
Q59 communicativeness ceiling (`+30%`); the multipliers compose
without saturating because `comprehension(...).min(Real::ONE)`
clips the final score.

**Bucket boundaries** are calibrated to the existing render
tiering: ring 0 (1 cell) = capital only; rings 0–2 (≤7 cells on
hex) include towns; rings 0–4 (≤19 cells) include villages; rings
5+ are hamlets. The 2/6/16 thresholds approximate where each new
tier-band starts contributing in the render's BFS-ring buckets.

**What this is not:**
- Not an authored building registry. There's no `Library`,
  `Granary`, `Aqueduct` enum. Settlements emerge from population
  spread, and persistence emerges from settlement scale.
- Not a per-building lifecycle. The simulation does not model
  individual structures, construction, or decay. Q61 captures the
  collective effect of settlement density, not individual buildings.
- Not coupled to carrying capacity. Q22's `tech_multiplier` is
  the productivity-tech axis (cultivation / irrigation / industrial
  agriculture) and remains orthogonal — settlements don't grow
  food, they preserve knowledge.

**Wiring.** New `Civ::peak_claimed_cells: u32` field, updated each
tick when `claim_cells(...)` is called (sim/core's territory phase).
New `Civ::settlement_persistence_multiplier()` method returns the
ladder above. `transmission::transmit_from_parent` multiplies it
into the comprehension score alongside the existing Q59
`comm_bonus`. Render-side settlement labelling is unchanged — the
per-cell BFS tiers in the post-run report are a complementary
display, not a duplicate calculation.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Emergence over authoring (AGENTS principle 2):* settlement
  scale derives from existing simulation state (`claimed_cells`),
  which derives from population, which derives from carrying
  capacity, which derives from planet substrate. No authored
  building list.
- *Species is the protagonist (principle 3):* a civ that grew
  large leaves more for the species canon to inherit. Successor
  civs founded in the wake of a sprawling predecessor recover
  more knowledge than those founded in the wake of a hamlet-
  bound predecessor.
- *Quantitative depth (principle 4):* settlements are now a
  measurable variable that affects transmission magnitude, not
  just a render decoration.

**Lives in:** `sim/civ/lib.rs` (`Civ::peak_claimed_cells` field +
`settlement_persistence_multiplier` method, `claim_cells` updates
the high-water mark), `sim/civ/transmission.rs`
(`transmit_from_parent` applies the multiplier),
`sim/report/src/render.rs` (per-cell BFS settlement labels —
unchanged, complementary).

**Follow-ups.**
- If cross-seed audit shows the 0.85 / 1.00 / 1.15 / 1.30 ladder
  collapses or saturates the transmission distribution, retune
  under Q28's umbrella.
- A "monumental archive" event (`SettlementMilestoneReached`?)
  would let the post-run report mark the year a civ crossed each
  scale boundary. Deferred until a consumer asks.
