## Q80 — Calendar from planet orbit
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** `protocol::MONTHS_PER_YEAR = 12` was hardcoded across
the codebase. Q72 seasonal templates `MonthIn(start, end)` modulo
by it; the post-run report renders "year N" by dividing tick count
by it; year-bearing constants like `STAGNATION_THRESHOLD_TICKS =
1000 years` multiply by it. A 16-month-orbit planet running
seasonal templates at a 12-month cadence fires winter / summer at
the wrong fraction of its actual year.

**Decision:** Add `Planet::orbital_period_months` (sampled in
[8, 16] across habitable worlds) and route Q72's seasonal-template
`MonthIn` modulo through it. The sim cadence stays Q67's
1-tick = 1-species-month — what changes is *seasonal alignment*,
not internal tick semantics.

**What does not change.** Year-bearing constants
(`STAGNATION_THRESHOLD_TICKS`, `TRANSCENDENCE_SUSTAINED_TICKS`,
`FOOD_CRISIS_STREAK_TICKS`, etc.) keep their `12 × X` ticks
calibration. They semantically mean "X-Earth-months of sustained
state," not "X orbital periods" — the value matters as a *tick
count* for cooldown / streak logic, regardless of what the planet
calls a year. Re-tuning each constant per-planet would compound
calibration uncertainty across the whole codebase without
delivering proportional vision payoff.

**What changes.** `Signature::MonthIn` modulos by
`ctx.orbital_period_months` (default 12 in `PlanetContext::earth_like()`
for unit tests). A 16-month world's `polar_winter` (`MonthIn(11, 1)`)
fires for 3 of 16 months of its orbital period instead of 3 of 12 —
a different fraction, but each planet's seasonal templates fire on
*its own* orbital cycle.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Different worlds, different sciences:* a Mars-like 16-month world
  experiences a different seasonal rhythm than an Earth-like
  12-month one. Civs on the longer-orbit world see longer winters
  in absolute tick count but the same seasonal *fraction* of their
  year.
- *Emergence over authoring:* `orbital_period_months` is sampled,
  not authored per-seed. Determinism preserved.

**Lives in:** `sim/world/src/lib.rs` (Planet field +
`sample_planet`), `sim/recognition/src/lib.rs` (`PlanetContext`
field + `MonthIn` modulo), `sim/core/src/lib.rs`
(`build_planet_context` populates it), `protocol/src/lib.rs`
(`PlanetDerived::orbital_period_months`).

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- Per-planet display calendar in the post-run report. Today the
  report shows "year N" using the universal 12-month standard
  (so a 16-month world's "year" in the report is 12 months —
  conventional but misleading). A future report-only follow-up
  could optionally render per-planet years.
- Sample `orbital_period_days` and `day_length_hours` correlation
  so flagrantly impossible combinations (1-year orbits with
  Mercury-style days) get filtered. Today both fields sample
  independently.
- A diurnal physics pass (Q-decision needed) would couple
  `day_length_hours` into per-cell temperature integration, then
  Q71's deferred `tidally_locked_terminator` template could fire
  on short-day worlds.
