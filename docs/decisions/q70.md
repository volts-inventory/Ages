## Q70 — Discovery cadence from species cognition
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Q3 / Q34 figures attempt hypothesis tests every
`attempt_period = 20` ticks per (template, channel) candidate.
That's hardcoded for every species regardless of cognition. A
species with cognition 0.9 attempts at the same rate as one
with cognition 0.1 — which contradicts the project's
"different worlds, different sciences" goal: cognitive species
should pursue scientific inquiry more aggressively.

Q70 derives `attempt_period` from `species.cognition`. Higher
cognition = shorter period = more attempts per tick across the
civ's candidate space → faster accumulation of confirmed
relations.

**Decision:** `attempt_period_actual = 20 × (1.5 − cognition)`,
clamped to a minimum of 5 ticks.

| `species.cognition` | `attempt_period_actual` | year-equiv |
|---:|---:|---:|
| 0.0 | 30 ticks | ~2.5 years |
| 0.5 | 20 ticks (Earth-like baseline) | ~1.7 years |
| 1.0 | 10 ticks | ~10 months |

A high-cognition species cycles through hypothesis attempts ~3×
faster than a low-cognition species. Per-civ throughput
(attempts × candidates / tick) scales accordingly. Confirmed
relations accumulate faster, civs reach plateau later, knowledge
transmission and the Q12 transcendence arc are more reachable
for high-cognition species.

The `streak_threshold = SUSTAINED_TRIGGER_TICKS.div_ceil(period)`
formula already adapts: shorter period → more attempts needed
to span the trigger window. The wall-clock confirmation time
for any single relation stays roughly constant; what changes is
*throughput* across the candidate space.

**Wiring:**

- `Hypothesizer::with_attempt_period(intelligence, templates,
  attempt_period)` — new constructor. Existing `new` calls the
  new constructor with the default 20.
- `Civ::with_species` derives the period from species cognition
  and passes it to figure construction.

**Determinism contract.** Pure function of seed-sampled
`species.cognition`; same seed = same period.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Different worlds, different sciences:* a cognition-0.9
  species' civ accumulates discoveries ~3× faster than a
  cognition-0.1 species' civ over the same number of years.
  Some civs reach Industrial Age in 1500 years; others take
  4500.
- *Quantitative depth:* discovery rate is now a measurable
  per-seed variable, not a flat constant.

**Lives in:** `sim/civ/discovery.rs` (new constructor +
parameterised default), `sim/civ/figures.rs` (passes period
through to figure hypothesizers), `sim/civ/lib.rs`
(Civ::with_species derives + threads).
