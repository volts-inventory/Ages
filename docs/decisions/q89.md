## Q89 — Report polish: scientific lifecycle counters per civ
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Q78 emits `RelationRevalidated` / `RelationLapsed`
when a successor civ's hypothesizer re-fits inherited knowledge;
Q79 emits `RelationFalsified` when an own law's predictions drift.
The events fire correctly (verified in their respective wave
PRs) but the post-run report doesn't surface them. The Q78 / Q79
follow-ups noted this as "low-payoff render churn" but the data
is exactly the kind a biography needs: did this civ's
inheritance hold up? Did its own theories survive contact with
new data?

**Decision:** Add three counters to `CivChapter`:
- `revalidated_count` — Q78 inherited relations the civ
  re-confirmed on its own data.
- `lapsed_count` — Q78 inherited relations the civ rejected.
- `falsified_count` — Q79 own laws whose predictions drifted
  past tolerance.

Increment in the digest's event-fold pass; render a
"Knowledge dynamics: revalidated N inherited laws, lapsed M,
falsified K of its own." line per civ. The line is omitted when
all counts are zero (e.g. inaugural civ before transmission
fires) so short-lived chapters stay terse.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Biography of a species:* the post-run report can now narrate
  "civ X graduated 11 of its parent's 14 laws and lapsed 3; in
  its lifetime it falsified 9 of its own theories." Knowledge
  evolution becomes legible in the chapter rather than only in
  the raw event stream.
- *Emergence over authoring:* the counters derive purely from
  events the simulation already emits. No new sim-side state.

**Lives in:** `sim/report/src/digest.rs` (`CivChapter` fields +
`absorb` increments), `sim/report/src/render.rs` (per-civ
chapter line).

**Deferred sub-items.** The Q77 follow-up "surface per-civ
founding-floor / carrying-capacity in the report" needs a
protocol change (the values aren't currently in `CivFounded`
events). Documented as a separate render-only follow-up; small
payoff, real protocol churn. Re-open if a worldbuilding pass
needs the substrate-derived demographics surfaced per chapter.

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- Inherited-from-civ attribution. The Q78 events carry
  `from_civ_id`; the report could group revalidations / lapses
  by source civ to render "civ X graduated 11 of civ Y's laws
  and lapsed 3 of civ Z's."
- Falsification timeline. Today's count is a sum across the
  civ's lifetime. A timeline of *when* falsifications fired
  would surface "civ X went through a paradigm crisis at year
  450" stories.
