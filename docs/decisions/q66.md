## Q66 — Multi-tick wars with a marching front
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Q10's `conflict::resolve` was a single-tick contest:
compute strengths, pick winner, drop loser pop in *all* disputed
cells uniformly, surrender *all* disputed cells if the loser
falls below `CONFLICT_DEFEAT_FLOOR` aggregate. One conflict
event = entire war resolved.

That doesn't read like real history. The Hundred Years' War
spanned 116 years; the Thirty Years' War 30. Border cells
flipped one at a time as the front shifted. Q66 makes the
existing per-pair conflict cadence (every `CONFLICT_CHECK_TICKS
= 75` ticks ≈ 6 years) play out a single skirmish round per
check, with cells flipping individually as their loser cohort
drains.

**Decision:** Keep the 75-tick check cadence; reduce per-check
loss fractions; flip cells one at a time when their loser
cohort drops below the defeat floor.

### Per-check flow

Each conflict check between civs `a` and `b` whose
`claimed_cells` overlap:

1. Compute strengths (`pop × literacy × Hierarchical`-bonus,
   unchanged from Q10).
2. Pick winner deterministically (higher strength; lower id
   ties).
3. **Reduced per-check loss fraction:** `loss_frac` floor
   stays `CONFLICT_MIN_LOSS = 10%` and ceiling stays at 50%
   for now — the headline numbers are preserved. The change is
   purely *which cells* take the hit and *when ownership
   flips*.
4. For each disputed cell:
   - Drop loser's region cohort by `loss_frac` (existing
     `drop_cell_pop` mechanic, but per-cell rather than uniform).
   - If that cell's loser cohort now sits below
     `CONFLICT_DEFEAT_FLOOR / 2 = 25` individuals, the cell
     flips: removed from loser's claim, added to winner's,
     winner seeds a fresh zero-pop cohort there. The flip is
     per-cell, so one check might flip 0, 1, or several cells
     depending on relative pre-check populations.
5. `loser_defeated` semantics shift: now it means "loser has
   no remaining overlap with winner" — i.e., this is the
   *last* round of the war. Single-cell wars still resolve in
   one check; multi-cell wars naturally take multiple checks
   to grind through.

### What this preserves

- The `ConflictResolved` event still fires on every check, with
  the same payload shape. Consumers reading the post-run report
  see one row per skirmish round; a multi-year war shows as
  multiple rows with progressively-shrinking
  `disputed_cell_count`.
- Q24 cosmology pushes (war drives Hierarchical / pushes
  Communitarian) still apply per check, so a long war keeps
  pushing both civs toward war-mindset cosmology — a real
  historical pattern.
- Q63's per-cell pop dynamics already track the granular cohort
  state Q66 reads; Q65's gradient migration may pull pop from
  interior cells *toward* contested border cells (defenders
  reinforcing the front) — emergent reinforcement.

### What this changes

- **Wars take multiple checks.** A war over 5 disputed cells
  won't resolve in one event; it'll span ~5-15 checks (30-90
  years equivalent) as the front marches cell-by-cell.
- **Stalemates emerge.** Two civs of similar strength take
  many rounds to flip even one cell; their war can stall at
  attrition equilibrium until one collapses for unrelated
  reasons (food crisis, plateau, catastrophe) and the survivor
  picks up the cells.
- **Reinforcement matters.** A civ whose interior is full of
  pop can shift pop to the front via Q65 migration, sustaining
  the war longer. A civ at low interior density runs out of
  defenders.

### What this is not

- Not a per-tick combat sim. Combat resolves at the
  `CONFLICT_CHECK_TICKS = 75` cadence; between checks pop
  steps + migration + catastrophes happen normally.
- Not a tactical layer (positions, supply lines, morale). The
  abstraction is "cells either flip or they don't this check."
  Tactical detail is out of scope per AGENTS.md.
- Not a refactor of `is_peaceful_pair` or Q10 diffusion —
  peaceful diffusion still checks the Hierarchical floor;
  hostile pairs run conflict checks.

### Why this respects the project goals

- *Emergence over authoring:* the marching-front reads as
  history because it derives from per-cell pop state +
  per-check strength comparisons. No authored war duration or
  outcome.
- *Different worlds, different sciences:* a high-cognition
  civ's literacy advantage compounds across many war checks; a
  low-cognition civ might initially have territorial advantage
  but lose the war of attrition. Same for sociality (mutual
  aid keeps cohorts alive longer under stress) and biosphere
  (richer planets refill border cells faster via Q65).
- *Quantitative depth:* `disputed_cell_count` over time
  becomes a measurable variable in the post-run report —
  long wars are visibly distinct from short wars.

**Lives in:** `sim/civ/conflict.rs` — revised `resolve()` to
flip cells individually based on per-cell loser cohort vs the
defeat floor.

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- Display: post-run report could group consecutive
  `ConflictResolved` events between the same pair into a "war"
  with start/end years and total cells flipped. Defer.
- War-driven catastrophe biases: famine + disease should fire
  more readily during long wars (siege starvation, refugee
  disease vectors). Defer.
- Naval / trans-water conflict: currently neighbours are hex
  axial only. Long-range warfare needs a different conflict
  model. Defer.
