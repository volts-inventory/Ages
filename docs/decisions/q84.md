## Q84 — Substrate-specific recognition templates
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Q82 added non-Aqueous substrates; Q83 made chemistry
substrate-relative. But the recognition catalog stayed Aqueous-
centric: a Silicate civ at 1100 K saw zero archetype-specific
templates because every existing one keys off water phase
transitions, water vapour, water depth, etc. Their species
literally couldn't perceive their own world's signature
phenomena.

**Decision:** Three new substrate-specific templates.

| ID | Name | Substrate | Signature |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 29 | `silicate_resonance` | Silicate | T 800-1500 K + |Charge| > 5 |
| 30 | `methane_seep` | Hydrocarbon | T 90-180 K + Fuel > 2 |
| 31 | `ammoniacal_storm` | Ammoniacal | T 195-240 K + Vapour > 0.5 |

Each fires only on the substrate's temperature window (the same
ranges Q82's `MetabolicSubstrate::temperature_range()` returns)
plus a substrate-relevant secondary signal:

- **Silicate resonance** keys on charge because Q82 biased
  Silicate toward Piezoelectric / RareEarth crusts (50/30/20%);
  silicon-substrate civs sense the crystalline lattice
  resonating in the EM field. Channels: tactile, seismic,
  electric-field, radio-native.
- **Methane seep** is the substrate-equivalent of `hydrocarbon_seep`
  for Hydrocarbon-substrate worlds — buried methane / ethane
  bubbling out of the crust. Channels: chemical-taste, chemical-
  pheromone, acoustic-air.
- **Ammoniacal storm** is ammonia-weather: cold cells with
  significant atmospheric vapour (NH3 cycle). Channels:
  chemical-taste, acoustic-air, tactile.

Verified end-to-end: Silicate seed 25 (rare_earth crust) fires
silicate_resonance 3360 times over 5 years; Hydrocarbon seed 3
fires methane_seep 2100 times; Ammoniacal seed 7 fires
ammoniacal_storm 120 times.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Different worlds, different sciences:* a silicon-substrate
  civ now has phenomena their species can perceive on their
  own substrate. The recognition catalog is no longer Aqueous-
  chauvinist.
- *Emergence over authoring:* no special planet-gating; the
  template's temperature window naturally constrains it to the
  right substrate (no Aqueous world reaches 800-1500 K).

**Lives in:** `sim/recognition/src/lib.rs` (templates 29-31),
`sim/species/src/lib.rs` (`template_channels` mirror for
29-31).

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- More substrate-specific phenomena. Today's three are an
  illustrative starter set; the catalog can grow as
  worldbuilding requirements emerge.
- Substrate-relative seasonal templates: Q72's `seasonal_thaw`
  is Aqueous-specific (273-290 K); a Silicate world might have
  its own seasonal solvus / liquidus events worth surfacing.
