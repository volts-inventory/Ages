## Q73 — Measurement relations: continuous-y law recovery
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Q34's discovery pipeline learns *firing relations*: each
sample is `(x = channel reading, y = did the template fire on this
cell, 0/1)`. That captures "the civ noticed lightning correlates with
charge" but NOT "the civ measured the underlying law". Confidence
maxes out as the threshold is crossed; the recovered parameter is a
threshold, not a coefficient.

The simulation actually has continuous laws — heat conduction's
`alpha`, EM's `conductivity`, gravity flow's `k` — that civs ought
to be able to recover from their surroundings. With binary y they
can't, no matter how cleverly we tune the templates.

Q73's question: how do civs learn continuous physical laws from
observing the world's actual fields, not just template firings?

**Decision:** Add a parallel **measurement relation** track to the
hypothesizer. A measurement relation samples
`(x = MeasurementChannel reading, y = MeasurementChannel reading)`
where both axes are continuous physics fields (or spatial
derivatives of them). Existing `fit::fit` machinery and `Form`
vocabulary apply unchanged — the only delta is where samples come
from.

**`MeasurementChannel` variants** (the smallest set that demonstrates
end-to-end law recovery; expand as later Q's demand):
- `Direct(Channel)` — read the channel value at the cell.
- `NeighbourMean(Channel)` — mean of the 6 axial neighbours.

A `NeighbourMean` x against a `Direct` y is a spatial-coupling
measurement. At diffusion equilibrium `T_cell ≈ mean(T_neighbours)`,
the linear fit recovers `slope ≈ 1` — the cleanest physics-grounded
"the civ measured a law" signal in the M2 physics surface.

**Default candidate set** (auto-seeded for every civ):

| y | x | What's recoverable |
|---|---|---|
| `Temperature` (Direct) | `Temperature` (NeighbourMean) | heat-diffusion equilibrium → slope ≈ 1 |
| `WaterDepth` (Direct) | `WaterDepth` (NeighbourMean) | gravity-flow smoothing → slope ≈ 1 |
| `ChargeMagnitude` (Direct) | `ChargeMagnitude` (NeighbourMean) | EM-diffusion smoothing → slope ≈ 1 |
| `Temperature` (Direct) | `Elevation` (Direct) | thermal lapse rate (if any) |

These are deliberately not "every (channel × channel) pair." Civs
that can't sense `ChargeMagnitude` (no electric-field modality)
shouldn't measure EM equilibrium — sensorium gating filters the
candidate list per Q47.

**`relation_id` namespace.** Firing relations occupy
`template_id × 16 + channel` (max ≈ 500). Measurement relations
get `1_000_000 + (y_disc × 256 + x_disc)` so they never collide.

**`MeasurementConfirmed` event** mirrors `RelationConfirmed`:
`{ tick, civ_id, figure_id, relation_id, y_channel, x_channel,
form, params, residual, confidence, n_samples }`. Reusing the same
fit-space → SI rescaling means the protocol stays Q52-clean.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Different worlds, different sciences:* a high-tilt rocky civ
  measures `T_cell ≈ neighbour_mean(T)` and recovers a real
  diffusion-equilibrium slope. A sub-surface ocean civ at uniform
  232 K sees zero gradient — the fit would emit `slope ≈ 1,
  residual ≈ 0` identically for any (x, y) pairing. Those
  degenerate fits are filtered at confirm time
  (`is_trivial_measurement` rejects samples whose variance on
  either axis is below `1e-4` in fit-space) so the species canon
  only carries laws derived from genuinely varying data. Civs
  living on uniform fields don't get to "discover" identity from
  noise.
- *Emergence over authoring:* no per-relation tuning. The pipeline
  observes; the fit module decides which forms clear `exp(-1)`.

**Lives in:** `sim/civ/src/discovery.rs` (new types + measurement
sample buffer + step), `sim/civ/src/lib.rs` (per-figure observe +
step), `sim/core/src/lib.rs` (event emission), `protocol/src/lib.rs`
(`MeasurementConfirmed`).

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- Temporal-derivative y (`dT/dt`, `dWaterDepth/dt`) recovers the
  diffusion *coefficient* not just the equilibrium relation — the
  next step toward "the civ measured Fourier's law from first
  principles." Needs the hypothesizer to hold a previous-state
  snapshot, which is a heavier wire-up than Q73's spatial
  measurements.
- `Laplacian` measurement channel for `∇²field` once the temporal
  derivative lands.
- Compositional relations: a confirmed `temperature ↔ x` relation
  becomes a basis term in downstream candidates. Lets civs build
  hierarchical theories rather than independent atomic relations.
