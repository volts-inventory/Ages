## Q76 — Compositional relations: confirmed laws as basis terms
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Every confirmed relation today is atomic. Civ A
confirms `temperature ↔ elevation`; civ A confirms
`temperature ↔ neighbour_mean(temperature)`; the two never
combine into `temperature = f(elevation, neighbour_mean)`. The
candidate space is purely (template × channel) — relations don't
become reusable predictors.

In real science, confirmed laws *do* compose. Once Newton has
`F = ma`, downstream theory uses `F` as a primitive — not re-
derived from observations every time. Without that hierarchy our
civs are stuck at one-relation depth forever.

**Decision:** Auto-generate **residual candidates** when a
measurement relation confirms. The new candidate fits the *residual*
of the source relation against a different channel.

**Mechanism:**
- New `ResidualBasis { source_relation_id, source_form,
  source_params, source_x_channel }` snapshots the confirmed
  relation at compose time.
- New `MeasurementCandidate::residual(y, x, basis)` constructor
  produces a candidate whose effective y is
  `direct_y - basis.source_form.evaluate(source_params, source_x)`
  — i.e., the residual after the basis's prediction is subtracted.
- When measurement R confirms, the hypothesizer auto-generates
  three child candidates pairing R's residual against
  `Direct(Elevation)`, `Direct(ChargeMagnitude)`, and
  `Direct(Fuel)`. The fit module decides which (if any) clear
  `exp(-1)` on the residual data.
- Child relation_ids live in the `2_000_000`+ namespace,
  disjoint from firing (max ~500) and direct measurement
  (`1_000_000`+) ids.

**Frozen basis vs. live re-evaluation:** the basis snapshot
includes form + params at compose time. Future Q35 refinement of
the source doesn't retroactively invalidate child fits. The
trade-off is documented: the hierarchy captures the civ's
*understanding at the moment of composition*, which is a more
faithful biography ("they thought T was linear in
neighbour-mean *then*, and built lapse-rate theory on top") than
a live re-evaluation that would force recomposition every time
upstream theory shifts.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Quantitative depth (Q4):* civs build hierarchical theories
  rather than independent atomic relations. `Temperature` first
  explained by `neighbour_mean(T)`; the residual then explained
  by elevation; the residual of *that* could be explained by
  charge — the start of a real Newton-to-Mercury-perihelion
  arc.
- *Emergence over authoring:* no per-relation tuning. The
  composition fires automatically when a base relation
  confirms; the fit module decides which residual fits
  successfully.
- *Biography of a species:* the post-run report can show the
  hierarchy ("civ X derived T-on-elevation as a *correction* to
  T-on-neighbour-mean").

**Pruning.** Today's implementation generates three residual
children per confirmed measurement (a manageable ~21 extra
candidates total in the default catalogue). The Q76 doc earlier
proposed a "only basis-compose if residual reduces by ≥ 10%"
heuristic; we ship without that gate for now and let the fit
module's `exp(-1)` confirmation threshold filter the noise.

**Lives in:** `sim/civ/src/discovery.rs`
(`ResidualBasis`, `MeasurementCandidate::residual`, residual
arm in `observe_cells`, auto-generation in measurement-confirm
branch).

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- Multi-level residual chains (residual of residual). Today's
  implementation generates one level deep; recursion would let
  civs build deeper theory hierarchies but compounds the
  combinatorial explosion.
- Live re-composition when source refines. Currently child
  candidates use a frozen snapshot; an alternative would
  re-fit children when their source refines (Q35).
- Residual-specific event tag in `MeasurementConfirmed` so the
  post-run report can render "civ X discovered: (T -
  predicted-by-neighbour-mean) ≈ -2.5 × elevation".
