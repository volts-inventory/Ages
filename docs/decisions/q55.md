## Q55 ŌĆö Sensorium-extending tech (latent ŌåÆ perceivable)
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** PLANNING M3 calls out "sensorium-extending tech (latent
ŌåÆ perceivable)" as a milestone deliverable. Q33's form-vocabulary
gate (Q53) needs an upstream story for what tech a civ has and how
that tech extends the species' default sensorium. Original sketch
was a hardcoded named ladder (`telescope`, `calendar`, `clocks`,
`scribal_math`, `microscope`); rejected because it bakes in
Earth-equivalence and breaks the "different worlds, different
sciences" project goal ŌĆö a sub-surface-ocean species would never
invent a calendar, but the hardcoded ladder would prescribe one.
**Decision:** Tools, not techs. A small set of physically-grounded
*tool categories* exist; each one is gated by present-physics
(planet supports the underlying signal) AND species capability
(species has the manipulation prereq). Tools grant **channels** ŌĆö
the same `ModalityKind` vocabulary species sensoria draw from
(Q29) ŌĆö and the union `species.modalities Ōł¬ civ.unlocked_channels`
drives recognition-template perceivability. As more channels
become available, more templates fire perceivably; as more
templates become perceivable, more form tags accumulate (Q53),
and the form vocabulary grows. No authored form-unlock table.

**Tool registry (M3, ~5 categories).** Each is
`(name, prereq_channels, prereq_manipulation, granted_channels,
tier)`. The list:

- `distance_imaging` ŌĆö extends visual reach. Prereq: species has
  `VisualLight`; manipulation `ToolExtension`. Grants: nothing
  channel-wise (visual already in set), but raises the *range*
  on the visual channel so far-field templates (when authored)
  fire. Tier 3.
- `remote_acoustic` ŌĆö extends acoustic reach. Prereq: species has
  `AcousticAir` or `AcousticWater`; manipulation `ToolExtension`.
  Same shape as `distance_imaging`: extends range on the existing
  channel. Tier 2.
- `field_sensor` ŌĆö synthetic electric-field sensing. Prereq:
  manipulation `ToolExtension`; planet supports `ElectricField`
  (atmosphere ŌēĀ None or aquatic). Grants: `ElectricField`
  modality for civs whose species lacks it natively. Tier 3.
- `thermal_sensor` ŌĆö synthetic infrared-thermal sensing. Prereq:
  manipulation `ToolExtension`. Grants: `InfraredThermal` for civs
  whose species lacks it natively. Tier 2.
- `magnetic_sensor` ŌĆö synthetic magnetic-sense. Prereq: planet
  has a `Magnetosphere` (Weak or Strong); manipulation
  `ToolExtension`. Grants: `MagneticSense`. Tier 4.

The list is small and physically-grounded; expansion authored when
new modalities or templates demand them.

**Tier identity (Q55 ŌēĪ Q28).** Q55's tool tier maps to Q28's
persistence tier on the same writing/printing/instrument-
sophistication axis. Per-tool alignment:

- T2 `thermal_sensor`, `remote_acoustic` ŌēĪ Q28 T2 (codified
  written-symbol systems; instrumentation begins).
- T3 `distance_imaging`, `field_sensor` ŌēĪ Q28 T3 (mass-distributed
  texts; precision optical / electrical instruments).
- T4 `magnetic_sensor` ŌēĪ Q28 T4 (mass-instrumented science;
  electromagnetic sensing is era-defining).

This is intentional alignment rather than coincidence: a civ that
preserves writing well also preserves the cumulative observation
record needed to design instruments at that sophistication.

**Orthogonality to Q22 productivity tech.** Q55 sensorium tools
extend *what a civ can perceive*; they do not change carrying
capacity. Q22's `tech_multiplier` is the productivity-tech axis
(cultivation, domestication, irrigation, storage, sanitation,
industrial agriculture per the Q22 spec) which is M4 work. The
two axes intentionally don't cross-couple in M3 ŌĆö a civ that
invents thermal sensors does not thereby grow its food supply.
M4's `tech_multiplier` lifts will come from the productivity
ladder, not from this tool registry.

**Trigger (M3 minimum).** Time-gated with prereq lockout. A tool
unlocks at `civ_age >= tool.tier ├Ś tier_unlock_period_ticks` if
the prereqs are met; otherwise permanently locked. `tier_unlock_period_ticks`
defaults to 200 (placeholder under Q28's tuning umbrella). Crude
but guaranteed to fire in M3 runs and naturally diverges species
because manipulation / channel prereqs lock species out of tools
they couldn't physically build. M4 (Q56) replaces the time gate
with observation-count + population ├Ś literacy.

**Events.** `TechUnlocked(tick, civ_id, tool, tier, granted_channels)`
emitted on each unlock so the post-run report can render a per-civ
sensorium-tech timeline.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Physical grounding (AGENTS principle 1):* tools gate on
  present-physics + species capability, not on Earth-equivalent
  names.
- *Emergence over authoring (principle 2):* form vocabulary
  emerges from perceivable-template tags, not from an authored
  per-tech form list.
- *Different worlds, different sciences (project goal):*
  sub-surface-ocean species without `VisualLight` never unlocks
  `distance_imaging`; species without `Magnetosphere`-supporting
  planets never unlocks `magnetic_sensor`. Two species reach
  different perceivable-template sets and therefore different
  form vocabularies and different discoveries.

**Follow-ups.**
- **Q56** (M4) ŌĆö tool-unlock triggers grounded in
  observation-count, population ├Ś literacy, manipulation
  proficiency rather than wall-clock ticks.
- Latent-template authoring (M3 starts with 5 templates, all
  tactile/visual-perceivable; richer templates that depend on
  `MagneticSense` / `RadioNative` / `VisualPolarization` make the
  channel-grant tools meaningful ŌĆö incremental as the recognition
  catalog grows).
