## Q88 — Multi-level residual chains
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Q76 generated residual children when a base measurement
confirmed. The civ's hierarchy went one level deep: T explained by
neighbour-mean → residual explained by elevation. But real theory
goes further — once temperature is explained by neighbour-mean
*and* lapse rate, the residual of *that* might still correlate with
charge (joule heating) or fuel (ecological feedback). The chain
should continue.

**Decision:** Make the auto-generation recursive and depth-capped.
`MeasurementCandidate` gains `residual_depth: u32`:
- Base catalogue candidates: depth 0.
- Q76 first-level children: depth 1.
- Q88 grandchildren: depth 2.
- Great-grandchildren: depth 3 (the cap).

When a candidate at depth D confirms, the auto-generation spawns
children at depth D+1 only if `D < MAX_RESIDUAL_DEPTH = 3`. The
candidate space stays bounded — without the cap a single base
measurement can spawn unbounded descendants over a long run as
relation_id collisions become rare.

The `residual()` constructor now takes `residual_depth` and mixes
it into the relation_id hash so the same (y, x) pair against
different bases / depths produces distinct ids. Without the depth
mix, depth-2 candidates from different parents could collide and
silently merge.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Quantitative depth (Q4):* civs build genuine theory hierarchies
  — three levels of residual decomposition give them the analog
  of "Newton's law explains 99%; the perihelion residual reveals
  general relativity." A worldbuilding pass with rich spatial
  variation across multiple channels could see civs land all
  three levels.
- *Emergence over authoring:* the depth cap doesn't pre-author
  which chains form; it just bounds the candidate growth. The
  fit module continues to filter trivial fits via Q73's
  `is_trivial_measurement` gate, so chains only deepen when the
  residual carries real signal.

**Verification.** Seed 42 / 100 years: 37 measurement_confirmed
events, 27 of them residual. Most chains terminate at depth 1
because seed 42's residual signal is small after the base
relation explains the bulk. Richer-substrate worlds (e.g.
Silicate seeds with crust-induced charge variation) would see
deeper chains.

**Lives in:** `sim/civ/src/discovery.rs`
(`MAX_RESIDUAL_DEPTH`, `residual_depth` field, depth-aware
`MeasurementCandidate::residual` constructor, depth-capped
auto-generation).

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- Per-substrate depth tuning. Aqueous worlds with little spatial
  variation rarely benefit from depth > 1; high-tilt rocky or
  Silicate/Piezoelectric worlds might benefit from 4-5.
- Post-run report rendering of the chain ("civ X derived: T
  ↔ neighbour_mean(T); residual ↔ elevation; residual of
  residual ↔ charge").
