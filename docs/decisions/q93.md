## Q93 — Lunar gravitational tides on the surface ocean
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Pre-Q93 the planet had no orbital companion in the
physics layer. `Planet::moon_count` existed as a worldgen field
but no law read it — surface water only changed via Q70 gravity
flow, Q92 hydrology, and the seeded initial state. Real planets
with moons see the surface ocean lift into a sub-lunar bulge and
an antipodal bulge (the gradient term in the lunar gravitational
potential), with the bulges sweeping the planet as the moon
orbits — twice-daily tides on Earth, much slower on slow
rotators.

Tides also need a *clock* that the existing Law trait didn't
expose. Pre-Q93 every law was stateless under `&self.integrate`
— there was no per-planet "what time is it?" reading. Adding a
genuine periodic forcing without a clock would force interior
mutability (a `Cell<u32>` per law) which both breaks the
"deterministic across parallel state instances sharing one law"
property and would have been a maintenance trap as more
periodic forcings landed (seasonal insolation, diurnal cycles).

**Decision:**

1. **Add a `macro_step: u64` counter to `PhysicsState`** with
   `state.macro_step()` and `state.advance_macro_step()`. The
   orchestrator advances it once per macro-step, after the law
   sequence runs. Laws that need a planetary clock (tides now;
   seasons / diurnal forcing later) read it.
2. **Add a `Tides` law** that:
   - Tracks `sub_lunar_q = (macro_step · width / lunar_period_macros) % width`
     using pure integer arithmetic — no transcendentals, no
     cell-time drift.
   - Computes a per-cell tidal potential `Φ[i]` with two peaks
     (sub-lunar and antipodal) at `Φ=+1`, falling triangularly
     to `Φ=-1` at the quarter-circle low-tide longitudes.
     Triangular shape rather than `cos(2θ)` because Q32.32 has
     no `sin`/`cos` and the triangle captures the same +1/0/-1
     phase relation that drives tidal flux at this resolution.
   - For each pair `(i, j)` with `i<j`: redistributes
     `water_depth` from low-Φ cells toward high-Φ cells via the
     standard pair-flux pattern (`flux = tide_k · dt · (Φ[i] - Φ[j])`,
     applied as `+flux` to `i` and `-flux` to `j`). Pair-flux
     preserves total water bit-exactly — the moon only *moves*
     surface water around, it doesn't create or destroy any.

`lunar_period_macros = 28` for any planet with `moon_count > 0`
(Earth's lunar cycle, given Q67's 1-day-per-macro-step cadence);
moonless planets get `lunar_period_macros = 0` which is a no-op.
Multi-moon mass-weighted superposition is deferred until
`Planet::moon_count` grows per-moon mass + period fields.

`tide_k = 0.001` keeps tidal range at ~0.1 % of column depth per
macro-step — matching the real-planet ratio of tidal range to
mean ocean depth (a few metres of tide on ~4-km mean depth) when
accumulated over a full lunar cycle.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Physics as close to real as possible:* lunar tides are a
  dominant short-period forcing on every Earth-like world.
  Q93 lets them emerge from `Planet::moon_count` and the
  macro-step clock instead of being modelled as static. The
  bulge sweeps continuously through the cycle — recognition
  templates that key on "high tide line" or "intertidal zone"
  become possible.
- *Least hardcoded values:* one tunable rate (`tide_k`), one
  derived period (`lunar_period_macros = 28` for any moon-bearing
  world). The triangular profile is the only hardcoded shape —
  refining toward `cos(2θ)` is a follow-up that needs Q32.32
  trig or table lookup.
- *Determinism:* `state.macro_step()` is the *only* state-
  derived input; pair iteration is canonical-order; no interior
  mutability; no per-tick allocation beyond a single potential
  vector. Two parallel state instances driven by the same
  `Tides` instance produce bit-identical results
  (`tides_are_deterministic`). Verified by
  `tides_conserve_total_water` for mass conservation.
- *Reusability:* the `state.macro_step()` clock is the right
  shape for every periodic forcing. Q94 magnetic field can
  read it for diurnal magnetosphere variations; future
  seasonal-insolation refinements to Q90 can read it for
  axial-tilt-driven sub-solar latitude shifts; diurnal day/night
  cycles read it for sub-tick rotation modelling.

**Numerical care.**
- `sub_lunar_q = (macro_step · width / period) % width` uses `u64`
  arithmetic with `saturating_mul` so a 10⁸-macro-step run on a
  1000-wide grid (10¹¹) doesn't overflow.
- `tide_k · dt · (Φ[i] - Φ[j])` is the largest hot-path product.
  With `tide_k=0.001`, `dt=1`, `|Φ[i] - Φ[j]| ≤ 2`, the per-pair
  flux stays under 0.002 — far inside Q32.32's ±2.1×10⁹.
- Defensive non-negative clamp on `water_depth` after
  redistribution; under earth-like coefficients this never
  triggers in practice but guards pathological `tide_k`.

**Lives in:** `sim/physics/src/tides.rs` (the law),
`sim/physics/src/state.rs` (`macro_step` field + accessors),
`sim/physics/src/lib.rs` (re-export),
`sim/physics/src/orchestration.rs` (wired into the macro-step
loop *first* so each pass starts by lifting the bulge before
GravityFlow / radiation / wind react;
`integrate_civ_step` takes `tides: Option<&Tides>` so
deterministic micro-tests can pass `None`; the orchestrator
calls `state.advance_macro_step()` at the end of every
macro-step iteration),
`sim/core/src/lib.rs` (`build_laws` returns an 8-tuple with
`Tides::for_planet(28)` for moon-bearing planets, `0` for
moonless).

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- *Multi-moon superposition.* `Planet::moon_count > 1` should
  produce interference patterns (Earth's moon + sun give the
  spring/neap cycle). Lands when `moon_count` becomes per-moon
  mass + period fields.
- *Cosine bulge profile.* A real `cos(2θ)` shape is smoother
  than the triangular approximation. Lands when Q32.32 trig
  arrives (table lookup with linear interpolation works
  deterministically).
- *r-axis (latitude) modulation.* Real tides also have a
  meridional (latitude) component because the moon orbits with
  some inclination. Lands when `Planet` carries an orbital-
  inclination field.
- *Tidal heating / friction.* Tidal flexing dissipates energy
  into the planet's interior — the source of Io's volcanism.
  Lands when interior-temperature tracking becomes a Q-decision.
- *Q93-aware recognition templates.* "Intertidal zone",
  "spring tide flooding", "tidal bore" all become recognisable
  now that the bulge sweeps continuously. Authoring lands when
  civs start accumulating tidal-cycle observations.
