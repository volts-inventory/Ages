## Q41 — Physics scope: which laws to encode
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Q40 commits to a physics engine but doesn't pin which
laws are in scope. Real physics is computationally infeasible at
planet scale; we need a deliberate subset. Five law-family options
(A–E) audited plus three flavours for the biology question (i/ii/iii).
**Decision:** Five law families, split across two milestones (option
E from the audit), biology indefinitely deferred (option iii from
the biology audit).

**M1a — mechanics + gravity + fluids + heat:**
- Mechanics + gravity (Newtonian; configurable inverse-power
  exponent — structural exponent stays fixed at 2, per Q44)
- Fluid dynamics (2D shallow-water equations on the regional grid)
- Heat conduction (diffusion + insolation + latent heat at phase
  boundaries)

**M1b — chemistry + simplified electromagnetism:**
- Simple chemistry (named substances + reactions + phase
  transitions + conservation)
- Simplified electromagnetism (charge accumulation, basic field
  propagation, magnetic-field structure from core + rotation)

**Indefinitely deferred (escape hatch only)**: biology primitives
(replication, metabolism, mutation as a continuous-physics layer).
The biology this project actually delivers comes from chemistry
(M1b) + cohort population dynamics (Q22, M3) + species derivation
(Q18, M3). If M3 prototyping demonstrates chemistry + cohorts can't
approximate some compelling biological phenomenon, biology
primitives get added as a delayed M1c. Otherwise they don't get
built.

**Permanently deferred**: turbulent flow, quantum effects, real
meteorology — computationally infeasible or out-of-scope.

**Why E over A**: M1a is meaningfully runnable on its own (planet
with atmosphere, oceans, thermal state), giving a validation
checkpoint before adding chemistry+EM coupling in M1b. Earlier
feedback, bounded blast radius, cleaner milestone tracking.

**Why iii over i/ii**: chemistry + cohort dynamics + species
derivation cover what civs need to observe and derive about biology.
Pre-committing biology primitives we may never use is high cost; the
escape-hatch lets us add them only if we discover we need to.
**Lives in:** `PLANNING.md` Milestones (M1a + M1b),
`docs/architecture.md` Physics engine section.
