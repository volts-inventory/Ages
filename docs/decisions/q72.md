## Q72 — Seasonal recognition templates
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Q67 wired seasonal physics — temperature varies per
cell per tick from `axial_tilt × temperature_gradient`. The
machinery is in place, but no recognition template currently
*observes* season. The species can't derive seasonal patterns
because nothing surfaces them as named phenomena. That defeats
the point of having seasonal physics in the first place.

Q72 adds 4 templates keyed off month-of-year × physics state.
Civs on high-tilt planets observe seasonal phenomena and fit
`Periodic` form to them, deriving "the year" as a measurable
quantity. Civs on tidally-locked or zero-tilt planets see no
seasonal templates fire — their species develops a different
relationship with time.

**Decision:** Add 4 templates that gate on `tick % MONTHS_PER_YEAR`
combined with physics state. Extend `Signature` with a new
`MonthIn(start, end)` variant that reads the current tick.

| ID | Name | When | Cell condition |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 25 | `seasonal_thaw` | months 2–4 | temperature 273–290 K |
| 26 | `polar_winter` | months 11–1 N hem / 5–7 S hem | temperature < 240 K |
| 27 | `equatorial_wet` | months 5–9 | water depth 0.5–3.0 m |
| 28 | `axial_extremum` | month 0 | temperature < 250 K |

Months are 0-indexed (`tick % 12`). `MonthIn(start, end)` with
`start <= end` matches `[start, end]`; with `start > end` it wraps
through the year boundary (e.g., `MonthIn(11, 1)` = Nov, Dec, Jan).

**Why these four.** Each captures a distinct seasonal mode:
- `seasonal_thaw` is **spring melt** — the moment ice retreat
  signals winter ending. High-cognition species fit `Periodic`
  forms to this and "discover the year."
- `polar_winter` is **deep cold** — fires only on cells already
  cold AND in winter months. High-tilt planets see it
  prominently at the poles for ~3 months/year; low-tilt planets
  see it never. Strong "different worlds" signal.
- `equatorial_wet` is **monsoon** — modest water during summer
  months. Captures rainy-season patterns on temperate planets.
- `axial_extremum` is **solstice** — the peak of either the cold
  or hot extreme, fires only in months 0 and 6. The most
  pronounced seasonal signal; species observe their planet's
  axial tilt directly through this.

**`Periodic` form unlocked for free.** All four templates carry
`FormTag::Periodic`. Q53's form-vocabulary derivation now picks
up `Periodic` for any civ that perceives one of them — letting
named figures fit sinusoidal/triangular forms to seasonal data.
Pre-Q72 only `harmonic_resonance` and `tidal_extremum` carried
Periodic; Q72 quadruples the path to that form.

**Determinism.** `MonthIn` reads `tick % MONTHS_PER_YEAR`, a pure
function of tick. Same seed = same firing pattern.

**`scan(tick)` signature change.** `RecognitionLibrary::scan`
gains a `tick: u64` parameter. Existing call sites (sim/core
ticks once per loop) thread it. The signature change ripples
through but is mechanical — tests use any reasonable tick.

**`sim_species::template_channels` mirror** updated for ids 25-28.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Different worlds, different sciences:* a high-tilt species
  develops a seasonal calendar early; a tidally-locked species
  doesn't (and so develops different time-keeping).
- *Quantitative depth:* civs can now confirm relations like
  `temperature ↔ tick (mod 12)` with a `Periodic` form,
  surfacing the planet's annual cycle as derived knowledge.

**Lives in:** `sim/recognition/src/lib.rs` (4 new templates +
`Signature::MonthIn` variant + `scan(tick)` signature change),
`sim/species/src/lib.rs` (`template_channels` mirror entries
25-28), `sim/core/src/lib.rs` (pass tick to scan).

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- Hemisphere-specific firing — landed. `Signature::Hemisphere` +
  `Signature::Any` ship in `sim/recognition`. `polar_winter` now
  fires northern cells during boreal winter (months 11/0/1) and
  southern cells during austral winter (months 5/6/7); shoulder
  seasons fire nowhere. Three regression tests in
  `sim/recognition/src/lib.rs` cover both winters and the
  silent-shoulder case.
- Day-length-keyed templates (diurnal cycles) — needs day-length
  to couple into temperature integration. Defer.
