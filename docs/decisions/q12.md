## Q12 — Run-end condition
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** When does a sim run terminate? With Q47, the species is
the persistent unit and civ-collapse is a normal mid-run event, not a
run-end. Run-end conditions are species-level.
**Decision:** Run-end taxonomy:
- **M3 ships with**: `species-extinction` (population truly zero
  across all regions, no civ active or stateless), `user-stop`,
  `fixed-horizon` (config-only safety cap, default ~10M ticks).
- **M4 layers in** (alongside civ lifecycle + culture):
  `catastrophe` (existential hazard at species-killing magnitude)
  and `stagnation` (no civ active and no founding triggers fired
  for many generations). Cultural-lock applies to *civs*, not the
  species, so it triggers civ-collapse, not run-end.
- **Multi-planet (deferred)**: `home-world-abandoned` (off-world
  presence + species pop = 0 on home planet).
- **Dropped entirely**:
  - `catalog-exhausted` — stale vocabulary (no catalog under Q40)
    and a leaky concept under Q32 refinement (civs never truly
    "exhaust" knowledge; refinement is open-ended). If we want a
    completeness measure for the post-run report, it lives there
    as a stat, not as a discrete event.
  - Singularity / substrate-transition / unified-theory — not
    honestly modelable without physics or cognition we can't
    credibly simulate.
- **Civ-extinction is NOT a run-end** — the species persists,
  T1+ artifacts persist, successor civs may emerge. Civ-collapse
  is mid-run drama, not termination.
**Lives in:** Will live in `docs/architecture.md` (validation /
run-loop) once code lands; per-Q reference in `docs/civ.md` Civ
lifecycle → Run-end and lifecycle.
