## Q71 — Planet-archetype-specific recognition templates
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** The recognition catalog (Q45) ships 14 templates that
are mostly Earth-equivalent — fire, lightning, ice, vapour, surface
water, flood zone, cold zone, fertile land, auroral activity,
harmonic resonance, static field gradient, tidal extremum, magnetic
field, thermal gradient. Of those, only `harmonic_resonance` is
genuinely non-Earth (piezoelectric crust signature).

The "different worlds, different sciences" goal (Q40) is half-
delivered: physics differs per planet, but the *vocabulary* of
phenomena is mostly familiar. Sub-surface ocean civs and gaseous-
shell civs (planets that should produce radically different
sciences) currently observe almost nothing — their physics state
doesn't trip the Earth-leaning catalog often enough.

Q71's question: which observable phenomena are intrinsic to
non-Earth planet archetypes, and what physics signatures encode
them?

**Decision:** Add 10 templates whose signatures fire reliably on
planet-archetype-appropriate physics state. No explicit planet
gating in the template definitions — the signatures themselves
encode the conditions that only co-occur on the right archetypes.
This keeps `RecognitionLibrary::earth_like_default` as the single
universal catalog; planet-specific firing emerges from the
underlying physics.

| ID | Name | Archetype | Signature |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 15 | `cryovolcanism` | Sub-surface ocean | high charge + ice present + cold |
| 16 | `ice_quake` | Sub-surface ocean | extreme charge in ice-rich cells |
| 17 | `pressure_storm` | Gaseous shell | extreme vapour + high temperature |
| 18 | `metallic_hydrogen_signal` | Gaseous shell | extreme charge + extreme temperature |
| 19 | `piezoelectric_pulse` | Piezoelectric crust | moderate charge + dry + fuel-rich |
| 20 | `magnetic_lodestone` | Ferrous crust | sustained moderate charge + dry land |
| 21 | `hydrocarbon_seep` | Hydrocarbon crust | high fuel + atmospheric vapour |
| 22 | `superconductor_resonance` | RareEarth crust + cold | extreme cold + moderate charge |
| 23 | `reducing_storm` | Reducing atmosphere | high vapour + high temperature + low oxidiser |
| 24 | `hazy_obscuration` | Hazy atmosphere | very high vapour + moderate temp |

**Why no explicit archetype gates.** The recognition layer scans
physics state per cell, agnostic to planet metadata. Hydrocarbon
crust deposits more `Substance::Fuel` per cell than other crusts
during `init_planet`; the `hydrocarbon_seep` template's
"high fuel + vapour" signature consequently fires more often there.
Same for the others — physics state IS the planet gating.

**Planet-property imprinting** (added during Q71). Without the
following, crust / atmosphere / magnetosphere / composition were
flavour-only and the templates wouldn't fire on the right
archetypes. `init_planet` now writes per-cell:

- **Crust → charge baseline on dry land:** Basaltic 0, Hydrocarbon
  2, Piezoelectric 12, Ferrous 15, RareEarth 6.
- **Magnetosphere → planet-wide charge baseline:** None 0, Weak 1,
  Strong 3.
- **Calibration is pinned by a regression test.** The crust ×
  magnetosphere matrix is brittle — every imprint must sit below
  `discharge_threshold_for(magnetosphere)` (else EM diffusion
  self-zaps tick 1) AND inside the firing window of the template
  it's calibrated for. `sim_world::tests::q71_imprints_satisfy_discharge_and_template_invariants`
  walks all 15 (Crust × Magnetosphere) pairs plus 3 GaseousShell
  variants and asserts both invariants. Retunes of either the
  discharge threshold or the template thresholds in
  `sim/recognition` now fail loudly instead of silently breaking
  the catalogue. Catching this surfaced one such collision:
  `superconductor_resonance` originally specified `|charge| in (5, 8)`
  but RareEarth + Strong magnetosphere produces 9, so the window
  was widened to `(5, 10)` (still excludes Hydrocarbon's max of 5
  and Piezoelectric's min of 12, preserving discrimination).
- **Atmosphere → planet-wide vapour baseline (above sea level):**
  None 0, Thin 0.05, Oxidising 0.2, Reducing 1, Hazy 2.
- **Composition → ice / vapour / temperature column:**
  - SubSurfaceOcean: water column reroutes to `Substance::Ice` on
    init (otherwise chemistry's first-tick freeze cascade dumps
    enough latent heat to re-melt the shell), water = 0.
  - GaseousShell: vapour 5, water = 0, fuel = 0, temperature
    floor 700 K, charge column tracking the magnetosphere class
    (15 / 35 / 70) so it sits below the EM discharge threshold
    (`build_laws`: 20 / 40 / 80) — otherwise the imprint
    self-zaps every tick.
  - Rocky / OceanWorld with mean temp < 263 K: 0.3 partial ice
    cover for cold-end planets.
- **Hydrocarbon crust → fuel bonus** bumped to +4 (was +2) so
  even a Sparse-biosphere hydrocarbon planet exceeds the
  `hydrocarbon_seep` threshold (4.0), while no non-hydrocarbon
  planet does (max non-HC fuel = HyperBiodiverse = 3.0).

**Channel + tag declarations** match each phenomenon's natural
sensorium and signal structure. Cryovolcanism (visual + tactile +
seismic + thermal); piezoelectric pulse (electric field + tactile);
hazy obscuration (visual only — limits it). Tags follow the form-
vocabulary expansion rule (Q53): templates with `Periodic` unlock
that form for civs that perceive them.

**`sim_species::template_channels` mirror** updated for ids 15-24.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Different worlds, different sciences:* a SubSurfaceOcean civ's
  observable inventory is now {fire (rare), ice_present (constant),
  cryovolcanism, ice_quake, harmonic_resonance, ...} — radically
  different from a Rocky civ's. They'd derive different physics
  laws because they observe different phenomena.
- *Emergence over authoring:* signatures are physics-grounded;
  templates fire when state crosses thresholds, not when a designer
  tags a planet "this fires here."

**Lives in:** `sim/recognition/src/lib.rs` (10 new templates),
`sim/species/src/lib.rs` (`template_channels` mirror entries
15–24).

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- A `tidally_locked_terminator` template for short-day worlds
  (day/night thermal extremum) — needs a tick-based or solar-flux
  signal that the current physics doesn't track. Defer until day-
  length couples into temperature integration.
