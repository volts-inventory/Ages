## Q74 — Relativise recognition thresholds to planet climate
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** The recognition catalogue uses absolute Kelvin
thresholds for many templates: `fire` requires temp > 500 K,
`cold_zone` requires temp < 250 K, `harmonic_resonance` fires
between 260 K and 310 K, `hazy_obscuration` between 260 K and
310 K. These are Earth-calibrated bands.

The vision (principle 1) says "no fudge constants where
derivation is possible" and "different worlds, different
sciences." A 200 K planet (sub-surface ocean) never observes
`harmonic_resonance` because no cell ever crosses 260 K. A 380 K
desert planet never observes a `cold_zone`. The bands silently
discriminate against non-Earth climates.

**Decision:** Templates that name climate-relative bands
(productive zones, polar zones, thermal anomalies) read their
thresholds from a `PlanetContext` passed to `scan`. Templates
that name absolute physical processes (water freezing point,
combustion ignition point, metallic-hydrogen depth) keep the
SI-absolute thresholds — they're physics, not authoring.

| ID | Name | Old threshold | New threshold |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | `fire` | T > 500 K | T > planet.ignition_threshold (already planet-derived in build_laws; surface that here too) |
| 7 | `cold_zone` | T < 250 K | T < `mean − temperature_gradient` (one full gradient below planet mean) |
| 8 | `flood_zone` | water_depth > 5 | unchanged (depth is physically grounded) |
| 9 | `fertile_land` | T 280–300 K | T within `± temperature_gradient/2` of planet mean (the productive band) |
| 10 | `harmonic_resonance` | T 260–310 K + charge > 0.05 | T in productive band + charge > 0.05 |
| 24 | `hazy_obscuration` | T 260–310 K + vapour > 1 | T in productive band + vapour > 1 |
| 25 | `seasonal_thaw` | T 273–290 K + months 2-4 | unchanged (273 K is water's freeze point — physical) |
| 26 | `polar_winter` | T < 240 K + months 11-1 | T < `mean − temperature_gradient/2` + months 11-1 |
| 28 | `axial_extremum` | T < 250 K + month 0 | T < `mean − temperature_gradient/2` + month 0 |

**`PlanetContext`** is a small immutable struct carrying just what
templates need: `mean_temperature`, `temperature_gradient`,
`ignition_threshold`, `surface_pressure`. Built once at run
start in `sim/core` from the sampled planet, threaded through
`recognition::scan(state, tick, ctx)`. No global state.

**`Signature::ProductiveBand`** is a new variant that reads the
context's mean ± gradient and matches when the cell temp is
inside. Replaces hand-written `Above + Below` pairs for the
relativised templates. Keeps existing absolute `Above` /
`Below` for true physical thresholds.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Different worlds, different sciences:* A 200 K sub-surface
  ocean's "polar winter" fires in cells colder than 188 K (its
  own gradient), not silently never-fires because 200 < 240.
  A 380 K desert civ's "fertile zone" sits at 360-400 K, not
  silently never-fires because 380 > 300. Bands are now
  *bands of this planet*, not bands of Earth.
- *Emergence over authoring:* The relativisation removes seven
  authored Earth-K thresholds. What's left (273 K freeze point,
  500 K combustion, 600 K metallic hydrogen) is real physics.

**Lives in:** `sim/recognition/src/lib.rs` (PlanetContext +
Signature::ProductiveBand + scan signature change), `sim/core/src/lib.rs`
(build context, thread through scan).
