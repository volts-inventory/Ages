## Q78 — Inherited-knowledge re-validation
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Q50 transmits confirmed relations from a parent civ to
its successor at founding, with a comprehension-decay factor based
on linguistic distance, age decay, and tier gap. The successor
receives the parent's relations as already-confirmed state — they
don't re-test the relations against their own observations.

That's a real fidelity gap. In actual history, inherited knowledge
gets re-validated by each generation: alchemy survived into the
modern era *partly* because its predictions weren't falsifiable;
once Lavoisier's chemistry made falsifiable claims, inherited
phlogiston theory died. Our successors silently accepted parent
knowledge regardless of fit on their own data.

**Decision:** Tag transmitted relations with inheritance metadata.
After a `REVALIDATION_WINDOW_TICKS = 50` tick window the successor's
hypothesizer re-fits the inherited form on its own observations:

- **Pass** (re-fit confidence ≥ exp(-1)): emit `RelationRevalidated`,
  graduate to native confirmed status (clear inheritance tags,
  reset `initial_residual` to the successor's own fit residual so
  Q79 prediction-tracking starts fresh).
- **Fail** (no fit clears): emit `RelationLapsed`, drop the
  relation from `confirmed`. The successor has rejected the
  parent's theory on its own data.

`ConfirmedRelation` gains `inherited_from_tick: Option<u64>` and
`inherited_from_civ_id: Option<u32>`. `transmit_from_parent`
populates them; `step_confirmed` checks the window expiry first
(before the existing residual / streak logic).

`HypothesisEvent::{Revalidated, Lapsed}` thread through sim/core
to `protocol::{RelationRevalidated, RelationLapsed}` events.
Cosmology pushes (Q24): revalidation pushes like a confirmation
(the civ has *measured* the law on its own data); lapse pushes
like a refinement-rejected (a piece of inherited theory no longer
holds; not a new finding either).

The streak counters (`falsification_streak`, `low_confidence_streak`)
reset on inheritance — the parent's drift state shouldn't bias the
successor's prediction-tracking.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Quantitative depth:* successors re-derive parent laws on their
  own observations rather than silently accepting them. Wrong
  inherited theory dies; right inherited theory strengthens.
- *Different worlds, different sciences:* a successor on a
  significantly different patch of physics (post-catastrophe
  changed climate, e.g.) lapses more inherited laws than one on
  a stable patch. The substrate signal extends through the
  inheritance pipeline.
- *Biography of a species:* the post-run report can now narrate
  "civ X inherited 14 laws from civ W; 11 graduated, 3 lapsed
  (the rejected ones were W's seasonal-thaw thresholds — civ X
  is on a hotter cell than W was)."

Verified on seed 42 / 1000 years: 653 transmitted, 575 revalidated,
54 lapsed, ~24 still in window at run end. The 8% lapse rate is
the expected signal — most parent knowledge survives but a
meaningful minority gets rejected on the successor's own data.

**Lives in:** `sim/civ/src/discovery.rs`
(`inherited_from_*` fields, `REVALIDATION_WINDOW_TICKS`, window-
expiry branch in `step_confirmed`,
`HypothesisEvent::{Revalidated, Lapsed}`),
`sim/civ/src/transmission.rs` (set inheritance tags on
transmit), `sim/core/src/lib.rs` (cosmology pushes + emit),
`protocol/src/lib.rs` (`RelationRevalidated`, `RelationLapsed`).

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- Per-civ revalidation tally in the post-run report ("civ X:
  inherited 14, graduated 11, lapsed 3").
- Combine with Q35: inherited relations could enter
  `RefinementState::Some(...)` instead of a separate window
  mechanism so Q35's existing probation machinery handles both.
  Skipped for now — Q35 probation is for *better forms*; Q78
  revalidation is for *the same form, new data*. Distinct
  semantics, separate code paths.
