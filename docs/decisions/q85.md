## Q85 — Substrate-specific Clausius-Clapeyron + per-substrate latent heats
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Q83 added per-substrate freeze/boil thresholds in the
chemistry layer. But two things stayed Earth-water-chauvinist:

1. Only Aqueous routed through `water_boiling_point_k` for
   pressure-aware boil; other substrates used a constant
   reference boil-point regardless of pressure.
2. All substrates used Earth-water's `L_FUSION_WATER` /
   `L_VAPORISATION_WATER` for the latent-heat term in phase
   transitions. A methane world's solvent freezing released the
   wrong amount of heat into the cell.

**Decision:** Generalise both.

`substrate_boiling_point_k(tag, pressure)` runs Clausius-Clapeyron
for *any* substrate — methane, ammonia, silicate now also get
pressure-varying boil. Sub-vacuum returns a sub-freeze sentinel so
chemistry treats the cell as evaporated.

`SubstrateProperties` carries the full phase + latent-heat profile
per substrate (freeze_point, boil_ref, l_fusion, l_vaporisation,
r_specific). `Chemistry::for_planet` reads it; the latent-heat
coefficients in the per-cell phase transitions now match the
substrate.

Per-substrate values:

| Substrate | freeze K | boil ref K | L_fusion (J/kg) | L_vap (J/kg) | R_specific (J/kg·K) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Aqueous (H2O) | 273.15 | 373.15 | 334 000 | 2 257 000 | 461 |
| Ammoniacal (NH3) | 195.4 | 239.8 | 339 000 | 1 371 000 | 488 |
| Hydrocarbon (CH4) | 90.7 | 111.7 | 58 000 | 510 000 | 518 |
| Silicate (Si) | 1687 | 3538 | 1 790 000 | 12 500 000 | 296 |

The c_p denominator in the K-per-kg conversion stays at water's
specific heat as a proxy for now — per-substrate `c_p` is a
follow-up refinement (each substrate has its own thermal capacity
that affects the per-cell temperature swing on phase transition).

`water_boiling_point_k` becomes a thin wrapper around
`substrate_boiling_point_k("aqueous", ...)` — backward compatible
for tests / legacy callers.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Physical / biological grounding:* methane's L_vap is ~25% of
  water's, so a phase change releases ~25% the heat. A Titan-style
  world's evaporation cycle now matches its actual chemistry, not
  Earth's water cycle.
- *Different worlds, different sciences:* civs measuring phase-
  transition latency see substrate-specific thermodynamics. The
  `delta_temperature ↔ laplacian_temperature` measurement (Q75)
  should now recover a substrate-correct heat-conduction
  coefficient on non-Aqueous worlds.

**Lives in:** `sim/physics/src/chemistry.rs` (constants,
`substrate_properties`, `substrate_boiling_point_k`,
`Chemistry::for_planet` rewire).

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- Per-substrate `c_p` (specific heat). The K-per-kg conversion
  currently uses water's c_p universally; methane's is ~2.2 ×
  water's, silicon solid is ~0.16 × water's. The denominator
  affects how much each kg of phase-transitioned substance moves
  the cell temperature.
- Substrate-specific cell thermal mass (`CELL_THERMAL_MASS_KG`
  is calibrated against water's specific heat at unit volume).
