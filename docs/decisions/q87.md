**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Q84 seeded the substrate-specific catalogue with three
templates (`silicate_resonance`, `methane_seep`, `ammoniacal_storm`).
Civilisations on those worlds finally had phenomena their species
could perceive on their own chemistry. But each substrate's
catalog was thin â€” one template per substrate. Q87 adds three
more so substrate biographies have richer perceivable inventories.

**Decision:** Three new templates extending the substrate catalog.

| ID | Name | Substrate | Signature |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 33 | `cryo_lake` | Hydrocarbon | T 90-180 K + WaterDepth > 0.5 m |
| 34 | `crystal_growth` | Silicate | T 800-1500 K + |Charge| > 8 |
| 35 | `aurora_polar` | cross-substrate | |Charge| > 10 + Cold band |

- **`cryo_lake`** fires on Hydrocarbon worlds where Substance::Water
  (semantically liquid methane on these worlds â€” Q83) accumulates
  to depth. Titan's methane lakes are the analog. Channels:
  acoustic-water (waves), tactile (cold liquid), chemical-taste
  (methane).
- **`crystal_growth`** fires on Silicate worlds where high-charge
  cells in the substrate's hot temperature window grow ordered
  crystalline structures. Distinct from `silicate_resonance`
  (id 29) which is a vibrational signal; `crystal_growth` is the
  structural / EM-field signal that drives silicon-substrate
  biology. Channels: tactile, seismic, electric-field.
- **`aurora_polar`** is cross-substrate â€” fires on any planet's
  cold polar cells with high charge. Distinct from the existing
  `auroral_activity` (id 11) which keys off charge alone;
  `aurora_polar` requires the cold band so it reads as a polar
  phenomenon rather than just any high-charge cell. Particularly
  visible on Ammoniacal worlds whose magnetospheres drive auroral
  ionisation in 195-240 K cells.

Verified across substrates: Hydrocarbon seed 3 fires `cryo_lake`
3185 times in 5 years; Silicate/Piezoelectric seed 27 fires both
`crystal_growth` (3720) and `aurora_polar` (251).

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Different worlds, different sciences:* a silicate civ now has
  *two* substrate-native templates to fold into observation
  pools (resonance + crystal growth); a hydrocarbon civ gets
  methane seeps + cryo-lakes; cold-polar worlds across substrates
  see auroral phenomena. The catalogue grows with substrate
  variety.
- *Emergence over authoring:* each template's signature is the
  natural physical condition; no per-planet gating.

**Lives in:** `sim/recognition/src/lib.rs` (templates 33-35),
`sim/species/src/lib.rs` (template_channels for 33-35).
