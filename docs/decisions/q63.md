## Q63 — Heterogeneous per-cell population dynamics
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** `region_cohorts: BTreeMap<u32, Cohort>` exists per
Q57 — each claimed cell carries its own `Cohort` — but the
population step ignores it. `step_population_with_capacity` runs
once on the aggregate `self.cohort` using civ-wide
`carrying_capacity`. Per-cell cohorts are mechanically reset to
`total / n_cells` on every `claim_cells` call, so every cell of
a civ has identical population at all times.

The post-Q62 viewport surfaces this as visibly artificial:
sprawling civs read as monolithic blobs because there's no
density gradient. Real civilizations have dense capitals + sparse
hinterlands — cells differ by an order of magnitude.

Q63 makes per-cell populations evolve independently so dense and
sparse regions emerge from physics, not authoring.

**Decision:** The population step iterates per-cell cohorts, each
using its own cell-local food security derived from cell-local
fuel density. Aggregate `cohort.count` becomes the *derived* sum
of `region_cohorts`, not an independent variable.

**Cell-local capacity.** `Civ::cell_capacity(state, cell)`:

```
cell_capacity = fuel_density[cell] × 50 × tech_multiplier
```

Restricted to one cell (the existing aggregate `carrying_capacity`
is the sum across `claimed_cells`). Seasonal modulation (Q67)
applies per-cell in the same step, so winter cells throttle
naturally.

**Per-cell step.** `step_population_per_cell(state, tick, planet)`:

1. Computes `cell_capacity × seasonal_factor` for each claimed
   cell once (snapshot of physics + season).
2. Steps each `region_cohort` independently with its own capacity
   under Q22 + Q68 dynamics (`stress_factor` from species).
3. Recomputes `self.cohort.count` as the sum of region cohorts so
   legacy callers (collapse triggers, transmission, anything
   reading aggregate population) see the right total.

**Territory expansion seeding.** `claim_cells` previously wiped
all cohorts and re-seeded uniformly. New behaviour:

- **Newly claimed cells** (in the new set, not in the old) seed
  from the centroid: 10% of centroid cohort transfers to the new
  cell, capped at 100 individuals so a single expansion doesn't
  drain the capital. Models "the capital sends colonists to the
  frontier".
- **Still-claimed cells** keep their existing cohort intact —
  the heterogeneous distribution survives expansion.
- **Shed cells** (in the old set, not in the new) drop out of
  `region_cohorts`. Their population becomes lost in the
  contraction (the simplest behaviour; Q65 migration later
  routes them to interior cells under contraction pressure).

**Why centroid seeding (not uniform spread).** "10% from centroid"
means the founding cell stays the densest cell long-term: the
capital seeds every frontier, so frontier cells grow from a small
base while the capital keeps its lead. After enough expansion,
density falls naturally with BFS-ring distance from the centroid
— mirroring real-world settlement patterns where capitals
dominate.

**Seasonal capacity per cell becomes meaningful.** Q67's
`seasonal_capacity_factor` was scoped to the aggregate civ but
the multipliers had to be conservative (1.0 / 0.92 / 0.80) to
avoid crushing the whole civ. With per-cell dynamics, seasonal
swings affect *only* the cells in their bite. A polar cell with
0.4× winter capacity now hurts only its own cohort, not the
whole civ. Q63 retunes the seasonal multipliers a touch sharper:
1.0 / 0.85 / 0.65. Equatorial cells stay productive year-round;
polar cells experience real winter pressure on local pop.

**Determinism contract.** `BTreeMap<u32, Cohort>` iteration order
is deterministic (cell-index sorted); same seed = byte-for-byte
identical event log.

**What this is not:**
- Not migration *between* existing cells. Q65 covers gradient-
  driven cell-to-cell flow under food-stress / war / catastrophe
  pressure.
- Not localised catastrophes. Q64 makes catastrophes hit specific
  cells rather than the whole civ uniformly.
- Not multi-tick wars. Q66 makes border conflicts march
  cell-by-cell over multiple ticks.

These (Q64–Q66) compose on top of Q63's heterogeneous state —
without per-cell density, none of them have anything to move
*between* or *contest over*.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Emergence over authoring (AGENTS principle 2):* density
  gradient emerges from cell-local fuel + the BFS-from-centroid
  expansion order. No authored "this cell is the capital" tag —
  the centroid just happens to be the cell every frontier seeds
  from, so it stays densest.
- *Physical grounding (principle 1):* cell-local capacity is the
  same fuel-density formula as the aggregate, restricted to a
  single cell.
- *Quantitative depth (principle 4):* per-cell population is now
  a measurable variable. Catastrophes (Q64) and wars (Q66) act
  on real cell-population values.

**Lives in:** `sim/civ/lib.rs` — `cell_capacity`,
`step_population_per_cell`, revised `claim_cells`, new tests.
`sim/world/lib.rs` — sharper seasonal multipliers (1.0 / 0.85 /
0.65) that work because Q63 isolates the bite per-cell.

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- Cross-seed audit after Q66 ships: confirm density gradient
  doesn't make capitals over-saturate. If it does, retune the
  10% / 100-cap centroid-transfer formula under Q28's umbrella.
