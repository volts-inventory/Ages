## Q67 — Tick = month + seasonal physics from axial tilt
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Until Q67 the project ran at one tick per species-year:
the per-tick physics integrator (then `integrate_civ_year`, now
`integrate_civ_step`) fired once per year, and every Q-tuned
threshold tied to `_TICKS` measured years. The yearly cadence
served the species-as-protagonist arc well — civs rise and fall
over thousands of years, and that arc reads at year resolution.

What it can't express:

- **Seasons.** The Planet event already carries
  `axial_tilt_deg` (sampled in `[0°, 45°]` at planet creation)
  and `day_length_hours`, but they're flavour-only — temperature
  is set once at `init_planet` and never varies in time. A planet
  with 30° axial tilt should have pronounced seasonal swings;
  a tidally locked planet with 0° tilt should not. The simulation
  has the *inputs* but not the *time resolution* to express the
  output.
- **Sub-year dynamics.** Multi-tick wars (Q66), inter-cell
  migration (Q65), and per-cell catastrophe impact (Q64) all
  read more naturally over months than over years. A war that
  resolves in "1 tick" at year cadence is one decisive year; at
  month cadence it can be a 24-month border campaign with the
  front line marching cell-by-cell — which is what the post-Q62
  viewport wants to *show*.
- **Seasonal capacity.** Carrying capacity is `fuel × 50 ×
  tech_multiplier`, computed once per tick. With seasonal
  variation, a temperate cell could hit 1.2× capacity in
  summer and 0.7× in winter; famine and food-crisis dynamics
  acquire a *season* rather than a uniform yearly rate.

Q67's question: what's the right tick unit, and how do we wire
the planet's existing seasonal inputs into time-varying physics
without breaking the determinism contract or the species-arc
calibration?

**Decision: 1 tick = 1 species-month, with `MONTHS_PER_YEAR = 12`.**

Months is the smallest unit that reads as "seasonal" in human
intuition (spring/summer/autumn/winter, ~3 months each), without
the 365× cost of day-grained ticks. Real-history calibration:
modern wars span 1–8 years (12–96 months); famines build over
3–10 years; epidemics last 1–4 years. All of these read better
in month-grained ticks than in year-grained.

**Cadence rules:**

- `integrate_civ_step` and the per-tick civ pipeline (cohort
  observations, hypothesis testing, refinement, lifecycle, etc.)
  continue to fire **every tick** (now monthly). The dynamics
  rates scale to preserve annual demographic equivalents.
- Year-meant time-window constants (`_TICKS`) scale × 12 so
  "100 years of food crisis" stays 100 years (now 1200 ticks).
- Population birth/death rates scale ÷ 12 so the same annual
  growth shape holds.
- Cross-civ check cadences (conflict every 75 ticks, diffusion
  every 100 ticks) **do not scale** — they stay at the same
  tick count. With tick=month, "every 75 ticks" reads as "every
  6 years", which is more realistic than "every 75 years" was.

**Seasonal physics (new).** A new
`seasonal_temperature_offset(tick, cell, planet, grid)` derives
a per-cell, per-tick offset from `mean_temperature`:

```
month_in_year = tick % 12
phase         = sin(2π × month_in_year / 12)        # [-1, +1]
hemisphere    = sign(cell_row - grid_height / 2)    # +1 N, -1 S
amplitude     = (axial_tilt_deg / 45) × max_swing   # K
latitude_band = pole_distance / half_height         # 0 equator, 1 pole

offset = amplitude × phase × hemisphere × latitude_band
```

`max_swing = 30 K` is calibrated so a 45°-tilt planet at the
poles swings ±30 K (Earth's polar swing is ~40 K seasonally; 30 K
gives generous-but-reasonable extremes without the model
collapsing under freeze cycles).

**Why this formula:**
- Equatorial cells stay nearly constant year-round (`latitude_band ≈ 0`).
- Polar cells swing the full amplitude.
- Northern and southern hemispheres run opposite seasons
  (`hemisphere` sign flip).
- Tilt-zero planets get zero offset (no seasons).
- Tilt-45° planets get full amplitude (extreme seasons).

The base temperature from `init_planet` stays in
`state.temperature()` unchanged; the seasonal offset is computed
on demand by callers that want time-varying temperature.

**Seasonal carrying capacity.** `Civ::cell_capacity(state, cell, tick)`
applies a seasonal factor:

```
cell_temp = base_temp[cell] + seasonal_offset(tick, cell, planet)
factor    = 1.0 if cell_temp ∈ [273, 303] K       # productive
          = 0.7 if cell_temp ∈ [253, 273) ∪ (303, 323] K   # stressed
          = 0.4 otherwise                          # extreme
```

Productive band 273–303 K (0–30 °C) is rough Earth growing-season
range; the falloff outside is steep enough to drive visible
winter / heat-wave effects without nullifying the cell.

**Determinism contract.** All operations remain deterministic-
arithmetic over `Real` (Q32.32). Same seed = byte-for-byte
identical event log. The seasonal offset is a pure function of
`(tick, cell, planet, grid)`.

**CLI ergonomics.** New `--years N` flag is sugar for
`--ticks N×12`. The existing `--ticks` continues to work as the
raw tick count for low-level callers; help text + README
examples migrate to `--years`.

**Report framing.** Year is still the user-facing unit: the
report shows "year 4321" not "tick 51852". The month component
appears in two places:

- **Catastrophe captions** ("year 4321 month 8 — volcanic
  eruption in cell 7") so seasonal context reads.
- **War events** ("year 4321 month 3 → year 4324 month 11")
  spanning a multi-year campaign.

Civ-arc events (founding, collapse, transcendence) keep year-
only framing — they're year-resolution semantically.

**What this is not:**
- Not a thermodynamic seasonal climate model. The offset is a
  derived display + capacity multiplier, not a re-integrated
  heat field. Q67 stays cheap.
- Not a refactor of physics integration. `integrate_civ_step`
  (renamed from `integrate_civ_year`) walks the same operator-
  splitting pipeline; the orchestration config's
  `macro_steps_per_step` (renamed from `macro_steps_per_year`)
  scales from 365/year to 30/month so the per-day macro-step
  cadence is preserved.
- Not a per-day cycle. Day length still influences flavour but
  doesn't drive any per-tick mechanic.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Physical grounding (principle 1):* seasonal swing emerges
  from `axial_tilt_deg` (a real planet sample) rather than
  authored "this planet has seasons, this one doesn't" tags.
- *Different worlds, different sciences (project goal):* a
  tidally-locked planet (tilt = 0) shows no seasonal capacity
  variation — its civs have flat year-round food security and
  develop differently from a high-tilt planet whose civs adapt
  to brutal winters.
- *Quantitative depth (principle 4):* capacity is now a
  `(cell, tick)` function, not a `cell`-only function.

**Lives in:** `sim/world/lib.rs` (`seasonal_temperature_offset`),
`sim/civ/lib.rs` (`cell_capacity`, revised `carrying_capacity`,
all `_TICKS` constants × 12, population-rate ÷ 12), `sim/civ/
catastrophe.rs` (cooldowns + cosmic-event firing × 12),
`sim/civ/tech.rs` (`TIER_UNLOCK_PERIOD_TICKS` × 12),
`sim/civ/transmission.rs` (`DECAY_CONSTANT_TICKS` × 12),
`sim/core/lib.rs` (`STAGNATION` / `TRANSCENDENCE` /
`SNAPSHOT_INTERVAL` × 12), `sim/population/lib.rs`
(`earth_like_default` rates ÷ 12), `ages/main.rs`
(`--years` flag), `sim/report/render.rs` (year/month captions).

**Follow-ups.**
- Seasonal recognition — templates that fire seasonally
  (auroral_activity peaks during high-tilt-pole winters,
  flood_zone biases to spring melt). Defer to a separate Q;
  needs the recognition catalog to grow first.
- Seasonal catastrophe biases — volcanic at any season, but
  drought biased to summer of low-rainfall years, ice-age
  biased to long-winter sequences. Defer.
- Tuning sweep — confirm civ arcs stay sensible after the
  rescale. Cross-seed run before merge.
