## Q75 — Temporal-derivative measurements (recover diffusion coefficients)
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Q73 added measurement relations that recover spatial-
*equilibrium* coefficients (`T_cell ≈ neighbour_mean(T)`, slope
≈ 1). That's a real law, but it's a property of the *steady
state*, not of the underlying *dynamic* law (`∂T/∂t = α∇²T`).
A civ that has measured Q73's smoothing relation knows their
planet is in thermal equilibrium; they have not measured `α`.

The actual heat-conduction law makes a falsifiable prediction
about how temperature *changes* between ticks. Recovering `α`
requires sampling `(x = laplacian(T at tick), y = ΔT between
tick and tick+1)`. Same for `EM::conductivity` (charge
diffusion) and `GravityFlow::k` (water flow).

**Decision:** Add `MeasurementChannel::Laplacian(Channel)` (the
discrete laplacian on the hex neighbourhood) and a per-cell
*delta* y that reads `(curr - prev) / dt`. The hypothesizer
holds a `prev_state: Option<PhysicsState>` snapshot taken at
each `PhysicsIntegration` boundary; observe samples
`(x = laplacian(prev), y = (curr - prev))`. A linear fit on
this pair recovers `α` directly.

**`MeasurementChannel::Laplacian`** computes
`Σ(neighbour - self)` over the 6 axial neighbours in fit-space.
Same scale as `Direct(field)` — fit-space coefficients are
unitless ratios.

**`MeasurementYKind::TemporalDelta(Channel)`** is a new y-axis
mode. Where `MeasurementChannel` (used for both x and y in Q73)
reads the same tick's state, `TemporalDelta` reads
`current[cell] - prev[cell]`. Requires the hypothesizer to
keep a previous-state snapshot; if no prev state is available
(tick 0), the sample is skipped.

**Default added measurements:**

| y | x | What's recoverable |
|---|---|---|
| `TemporalDelta(Temperature)` | `Laplacian(Temperature)` | heat-diffusion `α` |
| `TemporalDelta(ChargeMagnitude)` | `Laplacian(ChargeMagnitude)` | EM `conductivity` |
| `TemporalDelta(WaterDepth)` | `Laplacian(Elevation)` (gravity drives water down the elevation gradient) | gravity-flow rate `k × g` |

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Quantitative depth (Q4):* Civs now measure the actual
  diffusion coefficient, not just the equilibrium relation.
  A civ on a faster-conducting world derives a different `α`
  than a civ on a slower-conducting world — different physics.
- *Emergence over authoring:* No new authored thresholds.
  The fit module already supports `Linear`; the new
  measurements just give it temperature-evolution samples to
  fit.

**Snapshot strategy.** Cloning `PhysicsState` per tick is cheap
(96 cells × ~10 fields × Q32.32 = ~7.5 KB / tick), held in the
hypothesizer (one per civ-figure). Total at 30 figures × 7.5
KB = 225 KB sustained — fine.

**Lives in:** `sim/civ/src/discovery.rs` (Laplacian +
TemporalDelta + prev_state field on Hypothesizer + observe
hook), `sim/core/src/lib.rs` (call `hypothesizer.snapshot_prev()`
at the right phase boundary).

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- Confirmed measurements with `Laplacian(field)` ↔
  `TemporalDelta(field)` linear → publish `α` in real units
  via the existing `params_in_real_units` rescale (Q73 already
  handles dual-axis SI rescaling).
- A "predict + falsify" loop (Q79) that uses the recovered
  `α` to predict next-tick temperature and compares against
  observed — a confirmed law that consistently mispredicts
  triggers refinement.
