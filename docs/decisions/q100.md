## Q100 — Latent heat coupling in Hydrology
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Q92 shipped a hydrologic cycle with bit-exact mass
conservation: water_depth → Vapour (evaporation) → wind transport
→ water_depth (precipitation). But the *energy* side of the cycle
was unaccounted: the same latent heat that boils a kg of water
is supposed to be released when that vapour condenses elsewhere.
Pre-Q100 evaporation didn't cool the source and condensation
didn't warm the receiver — the cycle was thermodynamically
incoherent. Real climates use this latent-heat transport as a
*major* horizontal heat-flow mechanism (estimated ~80 W/m² at
mid-latitudes on Earth).

Chemistry already plumbs latent heat for the substance(Water) ↔
substance(Vapour) transitions; pre-Q100 those transitions were
de-facto unused (Q92 routes the dominant cycle through
water_depth instead). Replicating the same plumbing for the
water_depth ↔ Vapour path closes the energy budget on the cycle
that actually moves liquid around.

**Decision:** Add `lh_condense: Real` to `Hydrology`, derived
from the substrate's `L_vaporisation` divided by
`C_P_WATER × CELL_THERMAL_MASS_KG` (the same calibration
Chemistry uses, so Hydrology and Chemistry's latent-heat scales
match for any future cross-checks).

In the integrate path, apply latent heat alongside each mass
transfer:

```text
// Step 1 — evaporation: cool source cell
ΔT[i] = -transfer · lh_condense

// Step 3 — precipitation: warm receiver cell
ΔT[i] = +transfer · lh_condense
```

Sign convention matches Chemistry: positive `lh_condense` =
exothermic (warms the receiver). Evaporation is the reverse.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Physics as close to real as possible:* latent-heat transport
  is one of the dominant climate mechanisms on Earth. Tropical
  oceans evaporate (cooling), trade winds carry vapour
  poleward, mid-latitude clouds condense (warming) — gigawatts
  of energy redistributed without any sensible-heat advection.
  Pre-Q100 this entire mechanism was structurally invisible;
  Q100 makes it real.
- *Closes the energy budget:* every kg of mass moved by the
  hydrologic cycle now carries the right amount of energy. The
  cycle is no longer an energy-leak (mass conserved but
  enthalpy not).
- *Substrate-aware:* `lh_condense` is derived from the planet's
  metabolic substrate's `L_vaporisation`. Methane worlds release
  ~510 kJ/kg per condensation event; aqueous worlds release
  ~2257 kJ/kg; ammonia worlds ~1370 kJ/kg. The relative climate
  signatures of those worlds differ accordingly.
- *Determinism:* per-cell read of `temperature`,
  `water_depth`, `Vapour`; per-cell write of all three;
  `temps_next` initialised from `temps` and committed at the
  end of `integrate` so the per-cell ΔT contributions
  accumulate cleanly.

**Numerical care.**
- For aqueous: `lh_condense = 2_257_000 / (4_186 × 539) ≈ 1`
  K per kg of mass transferred. Per-tick transfer caps at
  `evap_rate × excess × water_depth = 1e-3 × 50 × 50 = 2.5`
  → ~2.5 K per tick at peak. Matches Chemistry's existing
  scale.
- The energy-applied product `transfer · lh_condense` peaks at
  ~5 (mass × K/mass) — far inside Q32.32's ±2.1×10⁹.
- Energy coupling adds a feedback: a hot wet cell evaporates
  → cools → boil threshold may now be above its temperature
  → evaporation slows. Self-limiting.

**Lives in:** `sim/physics/src/hydrology.rs` (new `lh_condense`
field, evap-cools / condense-warms application in `integrate`,
two new tests `q100_evaporation_cools_source_cell` /
`q100_condensation_warms_receiver_cell`),
`sim/physics/src/chemistry.rs` (`CELL_THERMAL_MASS_KG`
promoted to `pub const` so Hydrology can use the same
calibration).

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- *Per-substance specific heats.* Currently the cell thermal
  mass × c_p is a water-calibrated denominator. Methane worlds
  would benefit from substrate-specific c_p; tracked as a Q85
  follow-up.
- *Latent heat of fusion.* Q92 doesn't yet do
  water_depth ↔ Ice transitions (only chemistry does, on the
  substance pool). When Hydrology gains a freezing step, the
  same latent-heat plumbing applies with `L_fusion` instead of
  `L_vaporisation`.
- *Sub-boil saturation evaporation.* Even with Q100's energy
  closure, evaporation only fires above boil. Real evaporation
  has a saturation curve. Lands when per-cell humidity becomes
  a tracked field.
