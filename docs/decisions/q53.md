## Q53 — Functional-form unlock table
**Status:** Decided (no authored table; forms derive from perceivable-template structural tags)
**Context:** Q33 chose a tiered architecture for the 12-form
vocabulary — each form is gated by `available_forms(figure) ->
Set<Form>` at hypothesis-proposal sites — but ships M3 with a
placeholder gate that returns all 12 at T0. The original framing
was an authored unlock table mapping sensorium tech → forms.
**Decision:** No authored unlock table. Form availability is
*derived* from the structural tags on recognition templates the
civ currently perceives. Each `RecognitionTemplate` carries a
`tags: &'static [FormTag]` annotation describing the kind of
signal structure it produces (`Threshold`, `Periodic`, `DistanceDecay`,
`ExponentialChange`, `Logistic`, `Polynomial`, `Logarithmic`).
`available_forms(civ, species)` returns `{Constant, Linear}` (the
always-baseline forms) plus the union of `tag.forms()` over every
template the civ currently perceives. Sensorium-extending tech
(Q55) grows the perceivable set; new templates pull in their
form tags transitively.

Why not the authored table: hardcoding `telescope` /
`calendar` / `clocks` bakes in Earth-equivalence and breaks the
"different worlds, different sciences" goal. A sub-surface-ocean
species without sky access would never invent a calendar; under
the derivation rule, they reach `PeriodicSine` only if their
perceivable templates exhibit periodic structure (e.g. tidal
templates from a moon-driven sea), which is the right outcome.

**Lives in:** `sim/recognition` (`FormTag` annotation on each
template), `sim/civ/forms` (`available_forms(civ, species)`
derivation), `sim/civ/discovery` (consumer at hypothesis-proposal
sites). The Q55 Tool infrastructure feeds the perceivable-template
union on the civ side.
