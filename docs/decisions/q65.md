## Q65 — Gradient-driven inter-cell migration
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Q63 makes per-cell cohorts evolve independently;
Q64 makes catastrophes hit specific cells. But population
doesn't *move* between cells — a cell hit by plague stays
underpopulated forever (or grows back from its own residual);
a cell at capacity has no overflow valve to its less-populated
neighbours. That's not how real civilizations behave: refugees
flee struck cities; colonists migrate from capacity-saturated
cells to frontier hinterlands; trade routes drag pop along
corridors of food security.

Q65 adds a per-tick migration step: a fraction of each cell's
overflow + a fraction of each catastrophe-affected cell's pop
moves toward higher-capacity, less-stressed neighbours.

**Decision:** Each tick, after `step_population_per_cell`, run
a migration pass that moves pop from high-pressure cells to
low-pressure adjacent cells.

### Pressure metric

For each claimed cell, compute:

```
pressure(cell) = max(0, count - capacity * threshold)
```

with `threshold = 0.85` — population above 85% of cell capacity
generates migration pressure. Cells below threshold contribute
no outflow. (The 0.85 — vs 1.0 — gives migrate-before-stress
behaviour: people leave before food security drops, mirroring
real patterns of pre-emptive migration.)

### Flow rule

For each cell with `pressure > 0`:

1. Find its adjacent claimed cells (hex neighbours, intersected
   with `civ.claimed_cells`).
2. Compute each neighbour's *headroom* `= max(0, capacity -
   count)`.
3. If total headroom across neighbours is positive, move
   `min(pressure × MIGRATION_RATE, total_headroom)` from the
   source cell, distributed proportional to each neighbour's
   headroom.

`MIGRATION_RATE = 0.05` per tick — 5% of overflow leaves per
month. At yearly cadence that's ~46% of overflow per year, so
a saturated city with full overflow drains visibly to its
hinterlands within a few years.

### Determinism

`region_cohorts` is a `BTreeMap<u32, Cohort>`, so cell-iteration
order is deterministic. The migration pass uses two-phase
update (compute deltas first, apply atomically) so iteration
order doesn't bias the result.

### Composition with Q64

Q64 catastrophes drop cell pop. After Q64 fires, the *neighbour*
cells of the impact site have negative pressure (they're now
relatively low-density vs the still-claimed-but-dropped cell).
Q65's migration then naturally fills the impact crater from
surrounding cells over the following ticks — refugees drifting
back into the rebuilt zone. No special-case wiring needed; the
mechanic is the gradient.

### Composition with Q63 / Q68

Q68's species-derived stress factor still drives the
*step_with_capacity* coupling per cell. Q65 sits *between*
catastrophes and the next per-cell step: pop gets dropped at
specific cells (Q64), then migrates toward gradient (Q65), then
the next tick steps each cell's cohort (Q63). The three
mechanics compose naturally.

### What this is not

- Not war-driven migration (Q66 covers that). Border conflicts
  push pop away from contested cells; that's a war mechanism,
  not a peacetime gradient.
- Not seasonal nomadism. Cells already have season-varying
  capacity (Q67); migration follows that, but it's not a
  per-month directed cycle.
- Not inter-civ migration. People don't cross civ boundaries
  in Q65 — that's a separate question (Q70?) about asylum,
  refugee absorption, cultural assimilation.

### Why this respects the project goals

- *Emergence over authoring (principle 2):* migration follows
  the gradient field of (capacity − count) across the civ's
  claim. No authored "city A grows, hinterland B shrinks" tag.
- *Quantitative depth (principle 4):* per-cell density now
  tracks both birth/death AND migration; the viewport sees
  visible flow during catastrophes and post-war.

**Lives in:** `sim/civ/lib.rs` — new
`Civ::migrate_inter_cell(state, tick, planet)` method, called
from sim/core after the per-cell pop step.

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- Tunable `MIGRATION_RATE` per species (high sociality →
  faster migration, group cohesion). Defer to Q28 calibration
  pass.
- Inter-cell migration *across* civ borders during refugee
  events (post-Q66 wars). Defer.
