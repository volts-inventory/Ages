## Q81 — Parametric planet sampler (within-substrate correlation polish)
**Status:** Decided (within-substrate polish; full enum-replacement deferred)
**Context:** The original Q81 design called for replacing enum-
bucketed planet archetypes (Composition / Atmosphere /
Magnetosphere / Crust / BiosphereClass) with continuous parameter
vectors so the sampler could synthesise truly novel planet types.
That's a major architectural pivot — touches `sample_planet`, every
Q71 per-cell imprinting branch, Q74's `PlanetContext` derivation,
species derivation (Q47), the report (renders enum names), and
most of the Q-decision history.

Q82 (`MetabolicSubstrate`) took most of the air out of Q81's vision.
Substrate is now the meaningful variation axis; planets within a
substrate vary continuously in temperature / atmosphere / crust /
gravity / etc. The remaining Q81 value is *correlation polish*:
making within-substrate parameters covary in physically meaningful
ways instead of sampling independently from independent ranges.

**Decision:** Ship a narrow correlation pass — `temperature_gradient`
now covaries with `axial_tilt_deg`. The original ranges are
disjointly maintained but the *bracket* the gradient samples from
is interpolated linearly with tilt:

- tilt = 0° (no axial tilt): gradient samples in [5, 25] K
- tilt = 45° (high tilt): gradient samples in [20, 50] K

A high-tilt world has a wider equator-to-pole spread because solar
irradiance concentrates at the sub-solar latitude band. A low-tilt
world's gradient is gentler. This makes biographies feel more
physically grounded without re-architecting the sampler.

The sampling is integer-only (Q36) — no f64 round-trip in the
deterministic path. Pre-sample tilt as `i64`, derive the gradient
bracket via integer arithmetic, then re-use the same int for the
public `axial_tilt_deg` field.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Physical / biological grounding:* axial-tilt-driven gradient is
  real planetary physics. Earth at 23.4° has ~80 K equator-pole
  spread; Venus at near-zero tilt has ~10 K. The correlation
  surfaces this naturally.
- *Different worlds, different sciences:* a Mars-like high-tilt
  world will reliably have wider seasonal swings than an
  Earth-like one even before the substrate-relative templates
  fire. The substrate signal compounds with the
  tilt-correlated gradient.
- *Determinism:* integer arithmetic preserves Q36's "no f64 in
  deterministic paths."

**What is not done.** The full enum-replacement Q81 is still
deferred. With Q82 + Q74 + Q83 + Q84 the substrate-driven richness
is delivered through enum buckets that interact correctly across
the simulation. A future Q-decision can revisit if a worldbuilding
need surfaces that the current enum set can't express.

**Lives in:** `sim/world/src/lib.rs` (`sample_planet`).

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- Per-substrate temperature correlation with stellar luminosity
  (Q83's substrate windows are wide; high-luminosity worlds
  could skew toward the warmer end of their substrate band).
- Gravity / terrain_peak inverse correlation (high-gravity worlds
  flatten, low-gravity worlds tower).
