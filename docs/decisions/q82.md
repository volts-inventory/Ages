## Q82 — `MetabolicSubstrate` axis: every seed produces life of *some* chemistry
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Today's `BiosphereClass` enum (`None / Sparse / Lush /
HyperBiodiverse`) silently presumes Earth-water chemistry: the
sampler sets `BiosphereClass::None` whenever
`mean_temperature < 243 K` (water's lower liquid bound under
salt-suppressed conditions) or `atmosphere == None`. A 100 K
hydrocarbon-rich Titan-style world gets `None` because it's
"too cold for water" — but Titan is exactly where we'd expect
methane-substrate life. A 1500 K silicate world gets `None` for
"no atmosphere" — but silicon-substrate life doesn't need an
atmosphere of the Earth kind.

Q82 fixes both problems at once: the simulation samples a
**metabolic substrate** first (the chemistry life is built on),
then constrains planet parameters to *that substrate's* tolerance
window. Every seed produces a habitable world; what varies is
*which kind of biochemistry* the species runs on.

This also closes the user-flagged "why would life spawn on
inhabitable worlds?" question: it doesn't anymore. The
`BiosphereClass::None` outcome plus immediate `species_extinction`
at year 99 was game-mechanic theatre, not biography. Q82 makes
every run a real biography of *some* species on *some* substrate.

**Decision:** Add `MetabolicSubstrate` to `Planet`. Sample the
substrate first; sample compatible temperature, atmosphere,
crust, biosphere afterward. `BiosphereClass::None` becomes
unreachable through normal sampling — every seed picks
`Sparse / Lush / HyperBiodiverse`.

**Substrates and their tolerance windows:**

| Substrate | Liquid range (K) | Atmosphere | Crust bias | Composition bias |
|---|---:|---|---|---|
| `Aqueous` | 250-400 | Oxidising / Reducing / Hazy / Thin | any | Rocky / OceanWorld / SubSurfaceOcean |
| `Ammoniacal` | 195-240 | Reducing / Thin | any | Rocky (cold) / OceanWorld (cold) |
| `Hydrocarbon` | 90-180 | Reducing / Hazy / Thin | Hydrocarbon (strong) | Rocky (very cold) / GaseousShell |
| `Silicate` | 800-1500 | any (incl. None) | Piezoelectric / RareEarth / Basaltic | Rocky (hot) |

Silicate is the only substrate that admits `Atmosphere::None` —
silicon-substrate life is hypothesised to survive in vacuum because
its crystal lattice doesn't need an atmospheric solvent. The other
three need *some* atmosphere; sampling guarantees one.

**Sampling distribution:**

Substrate biased toward Aqueous since it's the most common
solvent in the universe (~60%), with the rarer chemistries split
across the remaining 40%:

| Substrate | Sampling weight |
|---|---:|
| `Aqueous` | 60% |
| `Ammoniacal` | 15% |
| `Hydrocarbon` | 15% |
| `Silicate` | 10% |

This delivers ~60% Earth-equivalent Aqueous worlds (the run-of-
the-mill habitability that the report's report-pipeline tests
were calibrated against) plus a meaningful 40% rotation of
non-Earth chemistries that produce genuinely different
biographies.

**`Planet::is_habitable()`** is now trivially true (the sampler
guarantees it). Kept as a public helper for downstream consumers
that want to assert the contract; tests pin
`is_habitable() == true` for every seed in `0..256`.

**Q71 imprinting interaction.** Per-cell substance imprints
(charge, vapour, ice, fuel) are still keyed off Composition /
Atmosphere / Crust / Magnetosphere / `BiosphereClass`. Q71's
imprinting math doesn't change. What changes is *which combinations
get sampled* — a Silicate-substrate Rocky world won't have
Atmosphere=Oxidising so the Q71 vapour imprint stays low. The
substrate is the gate; the imprint formula is the consequence.

**Q74 PlanetContext interaction.** Q74's `mean_temperature` and
`temperature_gradient` come from the actual cell-temperature
distribution. With Q82's substrate-constrained sampling, a
Silicate world's PlanetContext lands at ~1100 K mean — the same
relative-band machinery that Q74 already runs (`Hot` band on the
warm-side cells, `Cold` band on the cool-side cells) just
operates around 1100 K instead of 290 K. No special-casing
needed in recognition; the climate-relative bands already adapt.

**Why this respects the project goals.**

- *Physical / biological grounding:* life is now substrate-
  specific. A 100 K methane world hosts hydrocarbon-substrate
  life; a 290 K rocky world hosts aqueous life; a 1100 K
  silicate world hosts silicon-substrate life. No more
  Earth-water chauvinism.
- *Different worlds, different sciences:* the substrate is on
  `Planet` so downstream Q-decisions can branch on it
  (substrate-specific recognition templates, substrate-relative
  chemistry physics, substrate-tuned report prose). This commit
  ships only the sampling pivot; the rest follow as separate Q's.
- *Emergence over authoring:* substrate is *sampled*, not
  authored per-seed. Same seed → same substrate, deterministic
  contract preserved.
- *Biography of a species:* every seed now produces a real
  biography. No more "year 99 species_extinction on uninhabitable
  world" theatre.

**Lives in:** `sim/world/src/lib.rs` (substrate enum, refactored
`sample_planet`, `is_habitable` helper), `protocol/src/lib.rs`
(`metabolic_substrate` field on `PlanetDerived`),
`sim/report/src/render.rs` (planet card mentions the substrate).

**Follow-ups (separate Q-decisions):**

- **Q83** — substrate-relative chemistry physics. Today's
  `Chemistry` law uses water's freeze (273.15 K) and Clausius-
  Clapeyron boil. Silicate worlds should use silicon's solidus
  (~1670 K) and methane worlds should use methane's freeze
  (~91 K). New `Substance` variants (`SilicateLiquid`,
  `Hydrocarbon`, etc.) plus per-substrate threshold tables.
- **Q84** — substrate-specific recognition templates.
  `silicate_resonance` (Piezoelectric crust + 800-1500 K),
  `methane_seep` (Hydrocarbon crust + Reducing atmos + cold),
  `ammoniacal_storm` (cold + ammoniacal vapour). Today's catalog
  is Aqueous-centric (water freeze/boil-derived).
- **Q85** — Q81 supersedes itself: the parametric planet sampler
  now has a meaningful axis (substrate). Continuous-parameter
  planet generation is still useful (e.g., per-substrate temp
  *within* a substrate's window), but the enum/parametric pivot
  is less urgent now that habitability has substrate-aware
  variation.
