## Q106 — Coriolis deflection from planetary rotation
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Real winds curve right in the northern hemisphere
and left in the southern due to the Coriolis force `F = -2m·Ω×v`
that arises in a rotating reference frame. Pre-Q106 the wind
law was axisymmetric — N and S hemispheres behaved identically.
Without Coriolis, real Hadley cells, geostrophic flows, hurricane
rotation, and mirror-symmetric storm patterns are all impossible.
The Q91 follow-up list had it: "Coriolis deflection. Real winds
curve right (N) / left (S) due to planetary rotation. Lands when
rotation period becomes a queryable per-planet field."

`Planet::day_length_hours` has been queryable since the M1a
foundation; Q106 finally wires it into a deflection law.

**Decision:**

1. **`Coriolis` law** runs after `Wind` each macro-step.
2. Per-cell `Ω_z` derived from latitude and a tunable
   `coriolis_k`:
   ```
   lat_angle = sign · π/2 · |signed_offset| / half_h
   Ω_z       = coriolis_k · sin(lat_angle)
   ```
   The latitude angle uses the Q103 hemisphere convention
   (positive in N, negative in S) so Coriolis and Lorentz
   deflect in the same rotational sense per hemisphere.
3. Apply the cross-product `Δv = -Ω × v` for horizontal v in
   2D:
   ```
   Δv_q = +Ω_z · v_r · dt
   Δv_r = -Ω_z · v_q · dt
   ```
4. **`Coriolis::for_planet(day_length_hours)`** scales the
   deflection coefficient by `24 / day_length_hours`. Earth
   (24h) gets the default `coriolis_k = 0.001`; a 12-hour
   fast-rotator gets 2× as much; a 48-hour slow-rotator gets
   half. Models the real `2·Ω` proportionality without
   exploding the explicit-Euler integration.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Physics as close to real as possible:* Coriolis is the
  reason Earth has hurricanes spinning counter-clockwise in
  the N hemisphere and clockwise in the S, and is essential
  to Hadley-cell circulation, jet streams, and trade winds.
  Q106 makes all of that emergent from rotation rate and
  latitude.
- *Per-planet rotation matters:* a slowly-rotating world
  (e.g. 48-hour day) sees weaker Coriolis → rounder, less
  organized storms; a fast-rotator (12-hour) gets stronger
  deflection → tighter storm rotation. All from
  `day_length_hours`.
- *Same shape as Q98 Lorentz:* the rotational kick on velocity
  is mathematically identical to the magnetic Lorentz force,
  just unconditional on charge and proportional to angular
  velocity instead of magnetic field. Both signed by the
  hemisphere convention Q103 introduced.
- *Determinism:* per-cell read of `(v_q, v_r)` and grid axial
  row; per-cell write of `(v_q, v_r)`. Pure trig + Q32.32
  arithmetic, no interior mutability.

**Numerical care.**
- Real Coriolis `2 · Ω = 1.45e-4 rad/s` with our 1-day
  macro-step gives Δv ≈ 12.5·v per tick — far past Euler's
  rotational stability ceiling. We absorb that into
  `coriolis_k` and accept that we underestimate the true
  Coriolis frequency. The qualitative behaviour (deflection
  right in N, left in S, scaled by sin(lat) and 1/period)
  is preserved; quantitative match would need an implicit-
  symplectic integrator (Q110 Boris pusher applies here too).
- `coriolis_k = 0.001` keeps the per-tick rotation < 0.001
  rad/tick — well inside Euler stability. With Wind's
  30%/tick friction, the system stays bounded.

**Lives in:** `sim/physics/src/coriolis.rs` (the law),
`sim/physics/src/lib.rs` (re-export),
`sim/physics/src/orchestration.rs` (wired after `Wind`,
before `Hydrology`; `integrate_civ_step` takes
`coriolis: Option<&Coriolis>`),
`sim/core/src/lib.rs` (`build_laws` returns 11-tuple with
`Coriolis::for_planet(day_length_hours)`).

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- *Boris pusher.* Implicit-symplectic rotation lifts the per-
  tick stability limit and preserves `|v|` exactly. Same
  follow-up as Q98/Q110.
- *Geostrophic-balance recognition templates.* "Trade-wind
  belt", "westerlies", "polar easterlies" all become
  recognizable now that Coriolis is in. Authoring pending.
- *Planetary-rotation feedback on tides.* Real Earth-Moon
  tidal interaction slowly slows rotation (lengthens the
  day). Far-future refinement.
