## Q77 — Substrate-derived demographics
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Several civ-lifecycle constants are flat Earth
defaults independent of planet substrate or species traits:

- `FOUNDING_MIN_POPULATION = 100` (`sim/civ/lib.rs`).
- `per_unit = 50` individuals per fuel-density unit in
  `carrying_capacity` (`sim/civ/lib.rs`).
- Migration `pressure_threshold = 0.85` (Q65, `sim/civ/lib.rs`).
- Birth-rate per-biosphere multipliers
  (`None=0.7, Sparse=0.9, Lush=1.0, Hyper=1.2`) — fixed
  regardless of axial tilt or stellar luminosity.

The vision (principle 1) wants no fudge constants where
derivation is possible. A long-lived species on a lush world
should tolerate a much smaller founding cohort than a short-
lived species on a sparse one. A high-gravity world's carrying
capacity per fuel unit is genuinely different from a low-gravity
one (more energy required per individual).

**Decision:** Replace each constant with a derivation from the
existing planet + species fields.

**Founding population.**

```
founding_min = 50 +
    biosphere_pressure(planet.biosphere) * 100 +
    cognition_penalty(species.cognition) * 50
```

Where `biosphere_pressure` is `{None: 1.5, Sparse: 1.0, Lush:
0.5, Hyper: 0.25}` (sparse worlds need more founders to survive
random shocks) and `cognition_penalty` is `1 - cognition` (low-
cognition species need more bodies to maintain knowledge).
Range: 50-200 vs. flat 100.

**Carrying capacity per fuel unit.**

```
per_unit = base_per_unit *
    (1 + 0.5 * biosphere_richness) *
    (1 + 0.5 * (1 - planet.gravity / 9.81).abs()) *
    cognition
```

Where `base_per_unit = 30` (was 50). Lush-biosphere worlds
support more individuals per unit; high-gravity worlds support
fewer (more energy cost); cognitive species use resources more
efficiently. Range: ~15-90 vs. flat 50.

**Migration pressure threshold.**

```
pressure_threshold = 0.6 + 0.3 * species.sociality
```

Highly social species tolerate more crowding (0.9); solitary
species flee earlier (0.6). Range: 0.6-0.9 vs. flat 0.85.

**Birth-rate biosphere multiplier.**

```
birth_factor = base_biosphere_factor *
    (1 - 0.2 * axial_tilt_factor) *
    luminosity_factor
```

Where `axial_tilt_factor = axial_tilt / 90` (high-tilt worlds
have harsher seasonal pressure on births) and
`luminosity_factor` clamps to [0.7, 1.3] of stellar
irradiance / 1361 W/m² (Earth nominal). Mild adjustment, not a
multiplier override.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Different worlds, different sciences:* A short-lived
  cognition-low species on a Sparse no-magnetosphere world now
  needs ~180 founders, not 100; their carrying capacity per
  fuel unit is ~20, not 50; their birth-rate base is 0.65, not
  0.7. Their entire demographic envelope is genuinely different.
- *Emergence over authoring:* All four constants now derive
  from already-sampled fields. No new authoring; less hardcoded.

**Lives in:** `sim/civ/src/lib.rs` (founding gate, carrying
capacity, migration), `sim/world/src/lib.rs` (helper for
biosphere_pressure / cognition_penalty if needed).
