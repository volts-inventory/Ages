## Q59 — Figure charisma + curiosity
**Status:** Decided (M4-minimal: per-figure scalars sampled at
birth; consumed by Q24 cosmology drift magnitudes and Q48-v2
charismatic-founder trigger)
**Context:** Q24 cosmology drift magnitudes scale with the
charisma of the figure who triggered an event ("more important
named-figure" → larger cultural impact). Q48-v2 wants a
charismatic-founder trigger ("a figure with charisma ≥ 0.8 can
found a civ even without a stateless remnant"). Both need a
charisma scalar on `NamedFigure` that's been missing.

**Decision:** Two scalars per `NamedFigure`, both in `[0, 1]`,
sampled at birth from the per-civ RNG stream:

- `charisma: Real` — scales drift magnitudes (Q24) and gates
  charismatic-founder trigger (Q48-v2 ≥ 0.8). Distribution
  uniform over `[0, 1]` for v1; M4 v2 may bias by species
  `communication_fidelity` (species that communicate well
  produce more influential figures).
- `curiosity: Real` — scales fit-attempt cadence in the
  hypothesizer once Q25 focus-priority wiring lands (high
  curiosity = more attempts per relation per cycle). For v1
  the value is sampled but unused; consumers in M4 v2.

Sampling lives in `figures::found_band` (initial band) and the
M4 follow-up that grows civs' rosters dynamically. Same
`(species_seed, civ_id, band_index)` → identical scalars; the
RNG stream that names figures also rolls these.

**Why uniform `[0, 1]`:** simpler than gaussian; the downstream
gates (Q48 0.8 cutoff, Q24 magnitude scaling) work over uniform
distributions without bias correction. M4 v2 may swap to a
biased distribution if reference runs show too few
high-charisma founders.

**Lives in:** `sim/civ/figures.rs` `NamedFigure.charisma` +
`NamedFigure.curiosity`; consumed by `sim/civ/cosmology.rs`
(Q24) and `sim/civ/lib.rs` Q48-v2 founding logic.
