## Q105 — Multi-moon mass/period + tidal superposition
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Q93 shipped lunar tides with one period — any
planet with `moon_count > 0` got a single 28-macro-step cycle.
Real multi-moon systems produce *interference* patterns: Earth's
moon × sun spring/neap cycle, Jupiter's moon system Laplace
resonance, etc. The Q93 follow-up list called this out:
"Multi-moon superposition. `Planet::moon_count > 1` should
produce interference patterns. Lands when moon_count becomes
per-moon mass + period fields."

**Decision:** Promote moons to a real per-instance config:

1. **New `Moon` struct in `sim_world`**: `mass_relative_x100`
   (i64, Earth's moon = 100), `orbital_period_macros` (u32).
2. **`Planet::moons: Vec<Moon>`** populated at worldgen
   alongside the existing `moon_count`. Multi-moon planets get
   different periods inspired by real Earth-system bodies (28,
   13, 79, 7) and randomly varied masses.
3. **`Tides::moons: Vec<MoonTide>`** replaces the single
   `lunar_period_macros`. The `integrate` path computes a
   per-cell potential as the *sum* of each moon's `cos(2θ_m)`
   contribution, mass-weighted:
   ```
   Φ[i] = Σ_moons (m.mass_relative · cos(2θ_m(i)))
   ```
   where `θ_m(i)` is the longitude angle from moon `m`'s
   sub-lunar point.
4. **`Tides::sub_lunar_q(moon_idx, macro_step, width)`**
   accepts a moon index instead of being implicit. Pair-flux
   structure unchanged — total water still bit-exactly
   conserved regardless of how many moons contribute.
5. **`build_laws`** maps `planet.moons` to `MoonTide` structs.
   Moonless planets pass an empty list and the law no-ops.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Physics as close to real as possible:* multi-moon planets
  now show real interference. A planet with two moons at
  periods 28 and 13 will see beat-period high tides that
  modulate over `lcm(28, 13) = 364` macro-steps — about a
  year of constructive/destructive interference.
- *Per-moon mass weighting:* a small inner moon plus a large
  outer moon produce asymmetric tidal range — the larger
  moon's bulge dominates, the smaller one beats inside it.
  Realistic and emergent from the per-Moon `mass_relative`
  field.
- *Determinism:* worldgen samples mass deterministically from
  the seed; integrate iterates moons in a fixed order; pair
  iteration unchanged. Same seed → same multi-moon planet →
  same tidal pattern.
- *Bit-exact conservation:* pair-flux structure is independent
  of potential function shape, so summing N moon contributions
  to `Φ[i]` doesn't break total-water conservation
  (`tides_conserve_total_water` still passes).

**Numerical care.**
- `cos(2θ_m)` is bounded in `[-1, 1]`; mass-weighted sum is
  bounded by `Σ |mass_relative_m|` ≤ ~5 for a 4-moon system
  with masses up to 1.2. `dt · tide_k · ΔΦ` stays inside
  Q32.32.
- Per-tick cost: O(moons × cells). For a 4-moon planet with
  100 cells, that's 400 cos calls per macro-step — bounded
  and well within Q101's trig-cost budget.

**Lives in:** `sim/world/src/lib.rs` (`Moon` struct +
`Planet::moons` field + worldgen population),
`sim/physics/src/tides.rs` (`MoonTide` struct + `Tides` with
`moons: Vec<MoonTide>` instead of single period;
`sub_lunar_q` takes moon index; `integrate` sums contributions),
`sim/physics/src/lib.rs` (re-export `MoonTide`),
`sim/core/src/lib.rs` (`build_laws` maps `planet.moons` →
`Vec<MoonTide>`),
`sim/civ/src/catastrophe.rs` (test fixture updated to include
`moons`).

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- *Per-moon orbital plane / inclination.* Real moons orbit
  with different inclinations; tidal bulge tilts accordingly.
  Lands when the grid grows a meridional axis.
- *Sun as a tidal body.* Earth's solar tide is ~46 % the
  lunar tide and creates the spring/neap cycle. Modelling
  it requires a "sun moon" entry with the planet's orbital
  period — a small wiring change.
- *Tidal heating.* Real tidal flexing dissipates energy into
  the planet's interior (Io's volcanism). Lands when interior-
  temperature tracking becomes a Q-decision.
- *Resonance recognition.* Templates that fire on multi-period
  beat patterns ("spring tide flooding") need cross-tick
  signature support.
