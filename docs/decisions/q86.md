## Q86 — Tidally-locked planets + terminator template
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Q72 hemisphere-aware seasonal templates fire on tilted
worlds; Q80 added per-planet calendars. But tidally-locked
rotators — worlds where one face perpetually faces their star —
were never modelled. Civilisations on Proxima b-style tidally-
locked worlds cluster at the *terminator* (the boundary band
between perpetual day and perpetual night) where temperature is
mild. Without this template the simulation can't tell that story.

**Decision:**
- `Planet::is_tidally_locked()` returns true when
  `day_length_hours >= 1000` (~42 Earth-days+ of "day"). The
  sampler weights ~5% of seeds into the
  `[1000, 4000]` hour range so tidally-locked rotators appear in
  the rotation as genuinely-different worlds.
- `PlanetContext::is_tidally_locked` carries the bool to
  recognition.
- `Signature::TidallyLockedTerminator` matches when the planet is
  tidally locked AND the cell sits at q ≈ width/4 or 3·width/4
  (the +/-90° longitudes from the sub-solar point — i.e. the
  terminator band).
- New template `tidally_locked_terminator` (id 32): terminator-
  band + ProductiveBand temperature.

The terminator-band check uses cell column index as a *longitude
proxy*. Sub-solar point is conventionally q=0; terminator is at
q=width/4 and q=3·width/4. Cells within ±1 of those columns
match. On a 12-wide grid that's 6 cells of terminator (3 per
side); they appear in the post-run report's keyframes as the
narrow band where civs live.

Verified seed 39 (day_length 3150h, tidally locked): the
`tidally_locked_terminator` template fires 2172 times across 5
years — terminator cells observe the perpetual sunset / sunrise
band their species perceives via vision + thermal gradient.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Different worlds, different sciences:* a tidally-locked civ's
  biography unfolds along a 1D habitable strip rather than a
  hemispherical band. Settlement patterns, migration, and
  observation pools land differently. Q72's seasonal templates
  silently never-fire on these worlds (no axial-tilt-driven
  seasons) — replaced by the terminator's physical phenomenon
  instead.
- *Emergence over authoring:* `is_tidally_locked` derives from
  `day_length_hours`. No per-seed authoring; sampling delivers
  ~5% of seeds with this archetype.

**Lives in:** `sim/world/src/lib.rs` (`is_tidally_locked` +
sampler bias), `sim/recognition/src/lib.rs`
(`TidallyLockedTerminator` signature + template id 32),
`sim/species/src/lib.rs` (template_channels for id 32),
`sim/core/src/lib.rs` (PlanetContext threading).

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- Diurnal physics integration (per-cell day/night thermal swing
  driven by `day_length_hours`) — for non-tidally-locked worlds
  with very short or very long days. The current Q86 is a
  template-only response to tidal locking; couples nothing into
  the heat law itself.
- Substrate-aware terminator habitability — Aqueous worlds need
  the terminator at liquid-water temperature; Hydrocarbon worlds
  at liquid-methane temperature. Currently the productive-band
  filter (Q74) handles this generically via climate-relative
  bands but a tighter substrate-aware filter could improve
  fidelity.
