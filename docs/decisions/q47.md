## Q47 — Species as the persistent unit; civs rise and fall within
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Real history has many civilizations rising and falling on
the same species (Sumer → Akkad → Babylon → ... → modern). Civ-
extinction is a normal mid-history event; species-extinction is rare.
Modeling the *civ* as the run's persistent unit (run ends on civ
extinction) is wrong — it loses dark ages, renaissance dynamics,
inter-civ knowledge transmission via artifacts, all the things that
make civilization-history rich.
**Decision:** Species is the persistent unit of a run. Civilizations
are bounded collectivities within the species — they found, run, and
often collapse. Successor civs emerge from the same species. A run
ends only when the species ends (extinction, transcendence, etc.).
Civ-collapse is a normal mid-run event. Knowledge survives collapse
through:
- Artifacts at persistence tier T1+ (physical objects in regions,
  available to whatever civ later inhabits the region)
- Surviving named figures (may join successor civs or die out)
- Surviving cultural-memory tokens (oral tradition, T0, decays
  rapidly without persistence)
Inter-civ knowledge transmission is gated by comprehension
(linguistic distance + cultural distance + age of artifact +
persistence tier).

`docs/world.md` Species section, `PLANNING.md` Milestones (M4 expanded).
