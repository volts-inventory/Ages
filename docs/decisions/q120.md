## Q120 — Species line in the viewport
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Q119 added the planet name + survivability badge.
But civilizations are made of *species*, and the viewport
showed nothing about who lives there. "Civ 1 founded" is
abstract; "Pyrrites founded civ 1" gives the reader a handle.

`Species` was also nameless — internal traits (cognition,
modalities, manipulation modes) were derived deterministically
from the seed, but never given a human-readable identifier.

**Decision:** Three changes:

1. **`Species::name`** generated deterministically from
   `planet.seed` via `species_name_from_seed()`. 64 creature-
   feeling stems (`Kelv`, `Tolak`, `Velum`, `Korin`, …) × 4
   plural endings (`ites`, `ans`, `i`, `ar`) → ~256 distinct
   species names. The seed is XOR'd with a different magic
   constant from the planet name so the two pools pick
   independently (a `Vela-c` planet doesn't mechanically imply
   a `Vela`-stem species).
2. **`SpeciesDerived::name`** field added to the protocol
   `Species` event so downstream consumers (viewport, report,
   future narrator) can use it.
3. **Fourth viewport card line** showing
   `{species_name} · {cog_tier}-cog · {primary_modality}/{primary_manip}`:
   - **`cog_tier`** — `low` (cog < 0.34), `med` (0.34-0.67),
     `hi` (> 0.67). Three-tier discretisation of the
     continuous Q28 cognition scalar.
   - **`primary_modality`** — short label for
     `species.modalities[0]`. All 15 `ModalityKind` variants
     mapped to ≤ 7-char labels (`vis`, `ac-air`, `chem`,
     `mag`, etc.).
   - **`primary_manip`** — short label for
     `species.manipulation_modes[0]`. All 12 `ManipulationKind`
     variants mapped (`tool`, `tongue`, `web`, `trunk`,
     `tent`, etc.).

Sample outputs (5 seeds tested):
```
Caelum-a · survivable
aqueous · oxidising · 2 moons
no mag · 55F · 200h · 27° · 14mo
Tolakites · low-cog · touch/tool

Glade-a · toxic-atm
aqueous · reducing · 4 moons
no mag · 170F · 32h · 45° · 13mo
Doranites · low-cog · touch/trunk

Mira-a · alien-chemistry
ammoniacal · reducing · 2 moons
strong mag · -85F · 46h · 8° · 8mo
Quilites · low-cog · touch/chem
```

All four card lines fit 32 cols — still inside portrait phone
terminal width.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Reader-friendly identification:* "Pyrrites founded civ 1"
  reads better than "civ 1 founded" and gives the reader a
  handle for screenshots and conversations.
- *Honest summary:* the species line tells the truth about
  the planet's inhabitants — cognitive ceiling, what they
  sense with, what they manipulate with. No prose; just the
  most-defining traits at a glance.
- *Pure-function naming:* same seed → same name, byte-for-byte.
  No state; no per-tick branching.
- *Backward compatible:* `Species`, `SpeciesDerived` get a
  `name` field that downstream consumers can ignore. The
  viewport degrades gracefully (skips the species line) until
  the `Species` event fires.

**Lives in:** `sim/species/src/lib.rs` (new
`species_name_from_seed` + `Species::name` field + wiring),
`protocol/src/lib.rs` (`SpeciesDerived::name`),
`sim/core/src/lib.rs` (`species_to_event` copies `name`),
`sim/report/src/viewport.rs` (`species: Option<SpeciesDerived>`
field; `Event::Species` absorbed into state; planet card now
4 lines; new `short_modality` / `short_manip` mapping helpers).

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- *Per-substrate name pools.* All seeds draw from the same
  species pool. Substrate-themed names (silicate gets
  crystal-feeling names, hydrocarbon gets oil-feeling) would
  add narrative texture.
- *Use species name in civ events.* "Pyrrites founded civ 1"
  in the post-run report would be more reader-friendly than
  the current civ-id-only render. Small report-side change.
- *Topology + sociality on the species line.* Currently we
  show cog + modality + manip but not `cognition_topology`
  (centralised vs distributed) or `sociality`. Either could
  fit if we abbreviate further.
