## Q48 — Civ founding triggers
**Status:** Decided (M4-minimal: stateless-pop + recent-remnant
trigger; charismatic founder, post-catastrophe, uncolonised
migration deferred)
**Context:** Q47 commits to civ founding as a mechanic; the specific
triggers (charismatic founder, institutional remnant,
post-catastrophe reset, uncolonised migration) need calibration.
The richer triggers depend on machinery that doesn't exist yet —
charisma needs Q59 (no figure-personality model), per-region pop
needs Q57 (settlement-level cohorts), catastrophe needs Q60 (no
catastrophe events). M4-minimum ships the trigger that *can* fire
today: a stateless cohort left by a recently-collapsed civ
(the Q49-output) above a population threshold founds a successor.

**Decision (v2 — Q59 charisma + Q60 catastrophe added; v1 trigger
unchanged):**

Two M4-min triggers, OR-combined:

1. **Stateless-pop + recent-remnant + minimum dark age.** A new
  civ founds when the species' stateless cohort (Q49-produced
  cohort with `civ_membership = None`) has population ≥ 100 AND
  at least one civ collapsed within the last 250 ticks AND the
  collapse was at least 50 ticks ago.
2. **Charismatic-founder + post-catastrophe.** A new civ founds
  immediately (no dark-age requirement, no remnant requirement)
  when the stateless cohort has population ≥ `FOUNDING_MIN_POPULATION
  / 2` AND a catastrophe (Q60) fired within the last 100 ticks
  AND the per-civ-id RNG roll places `charisma >= 0.8` for at
  least one of the founding-band figures the next civ would mint.
  Captures the historical pattern of post-disaster founders
  galvanizing survivors. The minimum dark age
  enforces a visible gap so M4 acceptance's "dark age" reads as
  more than a same-tick refound; age decay in Q50 transmission
  also gets actual ticks to operate on. Founds with the same
  cohort (re-tagging `civ_membership` to the new civ id), a fresh
  founding band of 2–3 figures (Q3) drawn from a fresh
  `NameGrammar` (Q3 phoneme grammar reseeded per civ id), and a
  fresh `Hypothesizer` per figure.

The "recent remnant" condition is the cooldown — a stateless
cohort sitting alone forever doesn't refound spontaneously
(needs charismatic founder or migration triggers, deferred).
This is the structural minimum that gives M4 acceptance: a
collapsed → stateless → re-founded → recover sequence within
a single run.

**On founding:**
- New `Civ` created with the next civ id.
- Cohort `civ_membership` re-tagged to the new id.
- New founding band (`found_band`) creates 2–3 figures with
  fresh per-figure Hypothesizers.
- Inherited grammar drift: M4 follow-up; v1 reseeds the grammar
  fresh from `(new_civ_id, species_seed)`. Q50 will read the
  predecessor's grammar for linguistic-distance comprehension.
- A `CivFounded{tick, civ_id, founded_from_tick, parent_civ_id,
  initial_population_q32, founding_figure_count}` protocol event.

**Deferred to v2:**
- Charismatic-founder trigger: needs Q59 figure personality.
- Post-catastrophe trigger: needs Q60 catastrophe events.
- Uncolonised-migration trigger: needs Q57 per-region pop.
- Inherited cultural-memory tokens (Q47 oral-tradition
  inheritance): needs Q9/Q24 culture machinery + Q50.

**Lives in:** `sim/civ` founding logic + tick-loop integration in
`sim/core::CivLifecycle` phase (after Q49 collapse check);
`protocol/` event definition.
