## Q64 — Localized catastrophes (cell-targeted impact)
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Q60 + Q57 ship five catastrophe kinds. Volcanic
already targets a specific cell via `drop_cell_pop(cell, frac)`
because the volcanic trigger is a per-cell physical signature.
The other four — disease, asteroid, solar flare, ice age — apply
uniformly across the civ's whole cohort.

That's not how real catastrophes work. Disease starts in one
city and spreads to neighbors; an asteroid impact wrecks one
cell and damages the surrounding ring; a flare is planet-wide
but already partially captured by Q67's seasonal sensitivity.
Q63's per-cell population dynamics now make cell-targeted
catastrophes meaningful: a disease in the densest cell drops
that cell's cohort hard, leaving the hinterlands intact and
producing a visible localized hit in the viewport.

**Decision:** Make catastrophes target specific cells per their
real-world physics. Three behaviours, applied per-kind:

### 1. Disease — origin + spread

Disease starts at the **densest claimed cell** (highest cohort
count) — that's where contagion has the most fuel. Pop loss:

- Origin cell: `2 × DISEASE_POP_LOSS` fraction (3% × 2 = 6%
  baseline).
- Adjacent claimed cells (within the civ's claim, neighbouring
  the origin on the hex grid): `DISEASE_POP_LOSS` fraction
  (3%).
- Other cells: untouched.

Models a plague hitting the capital first and spilling into the
surrounding towns — the geometry the Black Death actually
followed in 14th-century Europe.

### 2. Asteroid — impact + debris

Asteroid picks a deterministic random cell from `(tick, civ_id)`
hash — the impact site is unpredictable but reproducible per
seed:

- Impact cell: `2 × ASTEROID_POP_LOSS` (12% × 2 = 24%).
- Adjacent cells: `0.5 × ASTEROID_POP_LOSS` (6%) — debris,
  fires, dust.
- Other cells: untouched.

If the picked cell isn't claimed (asteroid hit unclaimed land),
the catastrophe still fires globally with the original uniform
fraction — civs feel the planet-wide aftermath even if they
didn't take a direct hit.

### 3. Solar flare — uniform (planet-wide)

A flare hits the entire atmosphere; every cell experiences the
same EM disruption. No cell-targeting; existing uniform
behaviour preserved.

### 4. Ice age — uniform with a Q67 seasonal nuance

An ice age is planet-wide cold, but Q67's seasonal capacity
factor already amplifies it per-cell: high-latitude cells
already had reduced winter capacity, and an ice age effectively
extends winter year-round. Q64 leaves the explicit pop-loss
fraction uniform but documents that the per-cell capacity
throttle (Q63 + Q67) carries the spatial story.

### 5. Volcanic — unchanged (already cell-targeted per Q57)

**Helper functions** (sim/civ/catastrophe.rs):

- `densest_claimed_cell(civ) -> Option<u32>` — scans
  `region_cohorts`, returns the cell with the highest count.
- `deterministic_cell_pick(tick, civ_id, n_cells) -> u32` —
  hash-mixes tick + civ_id to get a stable random cell index;
  used by asteroid for the impact site.
- `hex_neighbors(cell, width, height) -> Vec<u32>` — six axial
  neighbours on the hex torus, matching Q57's BFS topology.
- `apply_to_cell_and_neighbors(civ, state, center, center_frac,
  neighbor_frac, claimed_only) -> Real` — drops pop by the
  given fractions, returns total loss for the event payload.

**Determinism contract.** All cell selection derives from
`(tick, civ_id)` — no per-tick RNG state. Same seed = same
catastrophe geography.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Emergence over authoring (principle 2):* "the densest cell
  gets the plague" emerges from population state, not from a
  tag. "The asteroid hits cell X" derives from a hash, not a
  designer's pick.
- *Different worlds, different sciences (project goal):* a
  HyperBiodiverse civ with a capital that holds 60% of its
  population gets crushed by a plague targeting that cell, even
  though aggregate `DISEASE_POP_LOSS` fraction is the same as
  on a Sparse-biosphere civ where pop is more evenly spread.
  Different demographic structures → different catastrophe
  outcomes.
- *Quantitative depth (principle 4):* viewer of the viewport
  now sees the affected cells flicker / drop visibly, rather
  than the whole civ losing 5% uniformly.

**Lives in:** `sim/civ/catastrophe.rs` — new helpers, updated
`check_and_apply` for disease + asteroid branches.

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- Weather catastrophes (drought, flood) as new
  `CatastropheKind` variants — drought picks low-fuel cells,
  flood picks coastal cells. Defer until a consumer asks.
- Multi-tick disease epidemics (spread over 6-12 months rather
  than instant) — natural extension once Q66's multi-tick wars
  validate the multi-tick pattern.
