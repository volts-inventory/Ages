## Q79 — Predict + falsify
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Q34 confirms a relation when fit residual on a sample
window clears `exp(-1)`. Q35 refines when residual sustained-drifts
above `exp(-2)`. Both are *backward-looking*: they fit past
observations.

Real science is forward-looking: a confirmed law makes falsifiable
predictions about the next observation. Mercury's perihelion
didn't break Newtonian gravity until astronomers checked the
prediction against actual observations and found mismatch. Our
civs never explicitly test predictions — they fit and refine on
the same residual, never on held-out data.

**Decision:** Add a *prediction-drift* trigger that runs alongside
the existing confidence-streak trigger:

- `ConfirmedRelation::initial_residual` snapshots the fit's residual
  at confirm time.
- `falsification_streak: u64` counts consecutive ticks the live
  RMSE on fresh samples exceeds `1.5 × initial_residual`.
- When the streak hits `FALSIFICATION_TRIGGER_TICKS = 30` (≈ 2.5
  years under Q67's monthly cadence), emit
  `HypothesisEvent::Falsified { relation_id, template_id, old_form,
  streak_ticks }` and force-trigger Q35 refinement immediately
  (set `low_confidence_streak = SUSTAINED_TRIGGER_TICKS`).
- Reset `falsification_streak` after firing so each drift episode
  emits exactly one falsification event, not one per tick of
  sustained drift.

The drift threshold (1.5×) and trigger length (30 ticks) sit
between the existing confidence-streak path (residual at
exp(-2) confidence + 50 ticks) — Q79 fires on faster, sharper
drift than Q35 catches on its own.

**`RelationFalsified` protocol event** mirrors `RefinementProposed`
in spirit: a *narrative* event surfacing "the civ's law is no
longer predicting." Refinement still flows through
`RefinementProposed` after this fires. Cosmology pushes (Q24) treat
falsification the same direction as a refinement-proposed event.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Quantitative depth:* a confirmed law's *predictive accuracy* is
  now monitored, not just its fit residual. Sustained
  mispredictions kill the law sooner than the fit-quality path.
- *Emergence over authoring:* the trigger derives from the law's
  own confirm-time residual; no per-relation tuning.
- *Different worlds, different sciences:* a stable-environment
  civ rarely sees its laws falsified. A volatile-environment civ
  cycles through laws faster — the catastrophic-environment
  archetype literally produces a different *epistemology*.

Verified on seed 42 / 1000 years: 1074 falsification events, 248
refinements proposed (122 confirmed). Falsifications correctly
emit once per drift episode rather than once per tick of
sustained drift.

**Lives in:** `sim/civ/src/discovery.rs` (new `initial_residual`
+ `falsification_streak` fields, drift-check in `step_confirmed`,
`HypothesisEvent::Falsified` variant), `sim/core/src/lib.rs`
(emit + cosmology push), `protocol/src/lib.rs`
(`RelationFalsified` event).

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- Per-cell prediction events for the post-run report's biography
  layer ("civ X predicted lightning at cell Y but observed
  none"). High event volume; sample-based subset would balance
  cost.
- Substrate / cosmology-modulated drift threshold. A dogmatic
  civ might tolerate higher drift (slower to falsify own laws);
  an empirical/reformist one would falsify sooner.
