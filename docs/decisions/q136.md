## Q136 — Decisions index restructure
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** With 134 decided + 5 open + 5 superseded entries,
`docs/decisions/INDEX.md` had grown to 159 lines — a wall of
text. Q133/Q134's audit flagged this as a MEDIUM finding:
locating "what's the current focus" or "where did the viewport
overhaul live" required eyeballing the whole list.

**Decision:** Reorganise the index into themed sections that
match how readers actually use it. New layout:

1. **Header + how-to-use** (unchanged).
2. **Latest decisions (last 10)** — quick-find for recent work.
   Newest first; one-line entries with the decision title only
   (full descriptions live in the themed sections below).
3. **Open questions** (unchanged content; just promoted in
   reading order).
4. **Decided — sim & physics core (Q1–Q113)** — the big
   foundational block: world sampling, physics laws, recognition
   templates, species/civ models, knowledge plumbing.
5. **Decided — viewport & UX (Q114–Q132)** — every iteration of
   the live ASCII viewport. Compounds: each Q assumes the
   previous shipped.
6. **Decided — tooling & narrator (Q133–)** — out-of-process
   tools + schema infrastructure that other consumers (future
   LLM, dashboards, web UI) build on top of.
7. **Superseded** — archived inside a `<details>` block so the
   wall of "kept for history" entries doesn't compete for
   eyeball-space.

The boundaries (Q113/Q114, Q132/Q133) reflect *natural* phase
transitions in the project: Q90-Q113 was the physics-fidelity
era; Q114-Q132 was the viewport-polish era; Q133+ is the
tooling/narrator era. Future Q's get added to whichever section
they belong to.

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Reader-first.* Most readers want either "what just changed"
  (Latest section) or "where does X live" (themed sections).
  Both got dedicated structure.
- *Cheap to maintain.* Each new Q goes into exactly one section
  and the Latest list. Roll the bottom of Latest off when it
  exceeds 10. No category re-shuffling.
- *Minimal churn.* Open / Superseded sections kept their
  contents; just relocated within the doc. The Decided section
  split into three by Q-number range, no entries lost or
  re-numbered.

**Lives in:** `docs/decisions/INDEX.md` only.

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- *Per-section archive at >50 entries.* If the sim & physics
  core section keeps growing, future restructure may want to
  split it further (e.g. world / physics / recognition / civ
  sub-sections).
- *Generate the index from the per-Q files.* A tiny script
  could `grep -l Status` across `q*.md` and emit the index.
  Defer until manual maintenance becomes a problem.
