## Q68 — Species + planet derive demographic constants
**Status:** Decided
**Context:** Q67's audit surfaced that the seed determines content
(planet sample, species traits, civ founding bands) but the
*mechanics* of civ demography are still hardcoded Earth-like
defaults — every species on every planet uses the same population
rates, the same knowledge-decay constant, the same food-stress
amplifier. That breaks the project's "different worlds, different
sciences" goal: a long-lived octopus-like distributed-cognition
species should not crash under famine the same way a short-lived
solitary land mammal does, and a HyperBiodiverse Lush world should
not have the same demographic momentum as a Sparse one.

The placeholders are flagged Q28 ("tunable later") but enough of
them affect every tick that targeted derivation pays off
disproportionately.

Q68's question: which placeholders meaningfully derive from
existing seed-sampled inputs, and what are the formulas?

**Decision:** Three derivations, all from existing planet sample +
species traits (no new traits, no new authoring):

### 1. Population rates (Q22)

Currently `PopulationDynamics::earth_like_default()`:
- birth_rate = 3% / year (= 0.25% / month under Q67)
- death_rate = 2.8% / year (= 0.233% / month)

Q68 derives both from `(planet.biosphere, species.cognition)`:

**Birth rate** scales with biosphere richness. Food abundance
drives reproductive rate. Factors are close to 1.0 even on hostile
worlds — otherwise civs there crash through the founding-pop
floor before any chain forms.

| `BiosphereClass` | factor |
|---|---:|
| `None` | 0.7 |
| `Sparse` | 0.9 |
| `Lush` | 1.0 (Earth-like baseline) |
| `HyperBiodiverse` | 1.2 |

**Death rate** is a *discount only* on the Earth-like baseline.
High-cognition species reduce mortality (medicine, hygiene,
agriculture); low-cognition species stay at the current default,
not punished:

```
death_factor = 1.0 − 0.3 × cognition       (cognition ∈ [0, 1])
```

cognition = 0 → 1.0× (current default; no penalty).
cognition = 0.5 → 0.85× death rate.
cognition = 1.0 → 0.7× death rate.

Net spread: a high-cognition species on a HyperBiodiverse planet
runs at 1.2× births / 0.7× deaths — strong positive momentum. A
low-cognition species on a Sparse planet runs at 0.9× births /
1.0× deaths — fragile but not perpetually shrinking.

The asymmetry (death only goes down, never up; birth varies
both ways) is deliberate: the Earth-like baseline already
calibrated against seeds with low-cognition species, so making
those species worse than baseline crashes civs.

### 2. Q50 knowledge-decay constant

Currently `DECAY_CONSTANT_TICKS = 1000 × MONTHS_PER_YEAR` (e-fold
over 1000 years) — fixed for every species.

Q68 derives the e-fold time from `(species.lifespan_years,
species.sociality)`:

```
decay_years = 500 + lifespan_years × 5 + sociality × 1000
```

- Short-lived (10 years), low sociality (0.2): 500 + 50 + 200 = 750 yr
- Earth-like (60 years), medium sociality (0.5): 500 + 300 + 500 = 1300 yr
- Long-lived (200 years), high sociality (0.8): 500 + 1000 + 800 = 2300 yr

A 3× spread. Long-lived social species (octopus-like, elephant-
equivalent) preserve oral tradition far better across collapse
boundaries than short-lived solitary species do — a real-world
fact. `Species::transmission_decay_ticks()` returns the
month-grained value (Q67).

### 3. Q22 stress factor

Currently `step_with_capacity` hardcodes a `4×` death amplification
under food stress. Q68 derives it from `(species.sociality,
species.cognition)`:

```
stress_factor = 5 − sociality − cognition       (each ∈ [0, 1])
```

Range: 3.0 (high-sociality + high-cognition) to 5.0 (low both).
Earth-like balanced (0.5, 0.5) gives 4.0 — current default
preserved. A high-sociality species' mutual aid + a high-cognition
species' adaptive behaviour both buffer death amplification under
famine. A low-sociality + low-cognition species crashes harder.

`PopulationDynamics` gains a `stress_factor: Real` field;
`step_with_capacity` reads from `self` instead of the hardcoded
`Real::from_int(4)`.

**Wiring.**

- `Species::transmission_decay_ticks(&self) -> u64` and
  `Species::stress_factor(&self) -> Real` are pure functions.
- `PopulationDynamics::for_species(species, planet) -> Self`
  constructs the per-species rates + stress factor in one place.
- `Civ` construction (sim/core) replaces
  `PopulationDynamics::earth_like_default()` with `for_species(...)`
  by overwriting `civ.dynamics` after the Civ constructor runs
  (the field is `pub`).
- `transmission::age_decay(age_ticks, decay_ticks)` and
  `comprehension(linguistic_dist, age_ticks, decay_ticks)` take
  the decay constant explicitly; `transmit_from_parent` reads
  it from the parent civ's species.

**What this is not:**
- Not a re-tuning of Q49 collapse thresholds — those stay at
  their year-equivalent calibration. Q68 only touches the per-
  tick demographic step + the cross-collapse transmission
  decay.
- Not a per-cell variation. Q63 will introduce per-cell
  population dynamics on top of these per-species rates.
- Not a removal of `PopulationDynamics::earth_like_default()` —
  internal civ tests still use it, and it stays as a clear
  baseline (cognition 0.5 / sociality 0.5 / lifespan 60 yr).

**Why this respects the project goals.**
- *Physical / biological grounding (principle 1):* every derived
  value comes from an existing seed-sampled input (biosphere
  enum, species traits) — no Earth-fixed magic numbers slipping
  back in via the formulas. The formulas themselves are
  authored, but the *values* per-run are seed-derived.
- *Different worlds, different sciences (project goal):* a high-
  cognition long-lived species on a HyperBiodiverse planet runs
  the project's species-as-protagonist arc with very different
  demographic momentum, knowledge persistence, and resilience
  curves than a low-cognition short-lived species on a Sparse
  planet.
- *Quantitative depth (principle 4):* civ behavior under food
  stress, knowledge persistence across collapse, and
  demographic recovery from low population are now
  measurable variables that vary per-seed.

**Lives in:** `sim/species/src/lib.rs` (new
`transmission_decay_ticks` + `stress_factor` methods),
`sim/population/src/lib.rs` (`PopulationDynamics::for_species` +
`stress_factor` field), `sim/civ/src/transmission.rs`
(`age_decay` + `comprehension` parameterized; `transmit_from_parent`
reads species), `sim/core/src/lib.rs` (overrides `civ.dynamics`
post-construction; passes species through to transmission).

**Follow-ups (not blocking).**
- Cross-seed audit after Q63 lands: confirm the demographic
  spread doesn't push too many seeds into perpetual-fragile or
  perpetual-flourishing territory. Retune ladder bucket
  boundaries under Q28's umbrella if needed.
- Q69+ for the remaining placeholders called out in the Q67
  audit: catastrophe severity from biosphere/crust, discovery
  cadence from cognition, etc. Q68 is the highest-impact
  three; the others are individually smaller.
